UPI Cibiru Campus Becomes a Strategic Partner for the Great Teachers Summit 2025 in Malaysia, Supporting the Achievement of SDG No. 4 on Quality Education

Cibiru, October 2025 – Universitas Pendidikan Indonesia (UPI) Cibiru Campus reaffirmed its commitment to the internationalization and sustainability of education by becoming a strategic partner in the Great Teachers Summit (GTS) 2025 activity held at Sultan Idris Education University (UPSI), Perak, Malaysia, on October 2–4, 2025. One of the participants in the activity was Dr. Dede Trie Kurniawan, M.Pd., Lecturer of PGSD at the UPI Campus in Cibiru.

This international event carries the theme "Teachers as Changemakers for Sustainable Education," highlighting the role of educators as agents of change in realizing sustainable education in the global era. GTS 2025 also coincides with the Global Educator Award, which recognizes inspiring educators from various countries for their dedication and innovation in education.

The series of activities, which took place from 8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. local time, featured discussion sessions, workshops, and scientific forums focusing on two key issues in future education: Early Childhood and Childcare Education and Planetary Health Education. Both areas emphasize the importance of inclusive, relevant, and sustainable education, in line with Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) Number 4, namely "Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all."

The participation of UPI Cibiru Campus in this forum demonstrates the active contribution of Indonesian universities in global efforts to improve the quality of education and strengthen teacher competency as the main pillars of achieving quality education.

The GTS 2025 event is organized by Leave a Nest Malaysia in collaboration with the Malaysian Ministry of Education, and involves various international strategic partners, including Taylor's University, Universiti Sains Malaysia (USM), Impact Lab, IPSC, Malaysian STEM Teachers Association, Salman Mosque Trustees Foundation ITB, and the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) Cibiru Campus.

The organizers expressed their deep appreciation to all partners for their collaboration. "The support and dedication of various educational institutions, including UPI Cibiru Campus, are integral to the success of the 2025 Great Teachers Summit. This collaboration is a concrete manifestation of our shared commitment to creating meaningful, equitable, and sustainable education," said the GTS committee.

Meanwhile, Dr. Dede Trie Kurniawan, M.Pd., a participant representing the Elementary School Teacher Education Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru, expressed his positive impression of the event. "Participating in the Great Teachers Summit 2025 provided valuable experience in understanding how educators from various countries build sustainable and humanistic learning systems. This forum opened new insights into the importance of cross-border collaboration in developing teachers who are adaptive, creative, and have global character," he said.

Through participation in this activity, UPI Cibiru Campus is committed to continuing to expand its global academic network, strengthening its contribution to educational innovation, and playing an active role in realizing quality education for all (SDG's No. 4).
