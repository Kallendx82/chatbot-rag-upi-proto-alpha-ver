The Early Childhood Education Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru will be closed on November 27, 2024, to support the simultaneous regional elections.

Cibiru, November 27, 2024 – The Early Childhood Education Teacher Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) Campus in Cibiru has declared a holiday for classes on Wednesday, November 27, 2024, to support the implementation of the 2024 Simultaneous Regional Head Elections (Pilkada). This Pilkada includes the election of regents and governors in various regions, including the residences of students, lecturers, and staff.

The Head of the Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) Study Program stated that this holiday aims to provide an opportunity for all academics to exercise their right to vote. "As educators, it is important for us to set an example of active participation in building democracy. We encourage all students and lecturers to exercise their right to vote responsibly," said the Head of the Study Program.

The 2024 Simultaneous Regional Elections (Pilkada Serentak) are a crucial moment for regional development and the sustainability of democracy in Indonesia. Within the educational context, the Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) Study Program also seeks to raise students' awareness of their role as active citizens in political and social life.

Classes at PGPAUD will resume as normal on Thursday, November 28, 2024. The entire academic community is expected to utilize this holiday to fulfill their responsibilities as voters. "We believe that the participation of lecturers, students, and staff in this regional election is a tangible contribution to the nation's future," added the Head of the Study Program.

The PGPAUD Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru continues to be committed to supporting the development of student character who are not only professionally competent, but also active as citizens who care about democracy.
