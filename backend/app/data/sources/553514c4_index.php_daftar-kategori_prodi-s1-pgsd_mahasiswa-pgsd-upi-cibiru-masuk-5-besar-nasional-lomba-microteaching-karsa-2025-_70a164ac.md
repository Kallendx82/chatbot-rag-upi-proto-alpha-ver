UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education Students Rank in the Top 5 Nationally in the 2025 KARSA Microteaching Competition: Realizing Quality Education

Palangka Raya, December 1, 2025 The 2025 National Academic and Arts Competition (KARSA) held by the State Hindu Institute (IAHN) Tampung Penyang Palangka Raya once again presented the best talents from various universities in Indonesia. In the announcement of the top 5 nationally, the Microteaching Competition category was one of the categories that attracted attention because it featured prospective educators who were able to practice teaching skills creatively, communicatively, and innovatively.

Of the five best participants who made it to the national finals, two of them were students from the Indonesian Education University (UPI), including the central campus and campuses in the regions.

List of the Top 5 in the 2025 KARSA Microteaching Competition

Alivia Ata Dinarzah (Indonesian University of Education, Cibiru Campus)

Jenny (IAHN Accommodates Penyang Palangka Raya)

Rizky Ahmadil Anhar (University of Palangka Raya)

Tri Silviya Pujyunah (Indonesian University of Education, Cibiru Campus)

Yetmi (IAHN Accommodates Penyang Palangka Raya)

The success of the two UPI representatives in the microteaching branch demonstrates the students' competence in managing learning professionally, especially in the 21st century education era which demands the use of technology, innovative approaches, and effective pedagogical strategies.

The Meaning of the 2025 KARSA Microteaching Competition

The microteaching competition provides a strategic platform to test prospective teachers' abilities in designing lessons, managing classes, and delivering material efficiently within a limited time. The inclusion of UPI students in the top five nationally demonstrates the quality of education prospective teachers receive through the curriculum, mentoring, and professional learning ecosystem across UPI's various campuses.

This competition also strengthens efforts to improve the quality of education, in line with Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) number 4, namely Quality Education. National-level microteaching events serve as a crucial platform for preparing future educators who are competent, have character, and are able to compete globally.
