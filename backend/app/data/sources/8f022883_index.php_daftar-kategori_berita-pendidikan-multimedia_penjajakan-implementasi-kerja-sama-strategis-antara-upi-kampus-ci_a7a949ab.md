UPI CIBIRU CAMPUS AND CIBIRU WETAN VILLAGE EXPLORE STRATEGIC COOPERATION BASED ON EDUCATION AND DIGITAL TECHNOLOGY

UPI Cibiru - The Indonesian University of Education (UPI) Cibiru Campus conducted an exploratory visit to implement cooperation with the Cibiru Wetan Village Government on Monday, October 27, 2025, at the Cibiru Wetan Village Office. This meeting was attended by the Director of the UPI Campus in Cibiru, Prof. Karim Suryadi, M.Sc., along with the Heads of Study Programs, namely Ade Nurhidayatullah, S.Pd., MT (Multimedia Education), Mochamad Iqbal Ardimansyah, ST, M.Kom. (Software Engineering), and Anugrah Adiwilaga, MT (Computer Engineering). From the village government, the Head of Cibiru Wetan Village, Hadian Supriatna, SP, along with the village officials were present.

The event began with remarks from the Village Head, who outlined the village profile and various flagship programs, such as the Digital Village initiative , strengthening early childhood education, and efforts to reduce stunting. The village government also outlined its 2026 plans, including the construction of a Command Center as a digital archive center, advanced training for MSMEs, and the development of digital tourism and technology-based waste management. The village government hopes that the collaboration with UPI will help strengthen community capacity in realizing a smart and competitive village.

Meanwhile, UPI, through the Heads of Study Programs, presented various synergy plans, such as the "Programmer Village" program from the RPL Study Program to improve community digital literacy, creative content and product packaging training by the Multimedia Education Study Program, and Mikrotik certification training and village digital infrastructure development by the Computer Engineering Study Program. The Director of UPI Cibiru Campus also conveyed plans to develop a Social Laboratory and a Community Service (PkM) program in Cibiru Wetan Village as a concrete implementation of collaboration between the campus and the village.

The meeting concluded with a commitment from both parties to continue strengthening collaboration in education, technology, and digital-based community empowerment, with the spirit of building competitive and sustainable smart villages.

This activity supports SDG 4 (Quality Education) , SDG 8 (Decent Work and Economic Growth) , and SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities) through community capacity building, digital education innovation, and strengthening technology-based local economies. (Cbr, 10/28/2025).
