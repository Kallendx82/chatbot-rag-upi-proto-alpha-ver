Morning Memo: P3K Student Farewell Ceremony at UPI Cibiru Laboratory Elementary School

Monday, June 16, 2025. The P3K PGSD student group at the UPI Campus in Cibiru consisting of Ayu Rahmawati, Eva Patmawati, Kharisma Salsabila Sofa, Nabilahthul Fuadah, Septiyani Sallwa Shiny, Shalsabilla Aurellia, Siti Hawa, and Yohana RU Sianturi held an event called Morning Memo as a form of closing their service period at the UPI Cibiru Laboratory Elementary School. This event was attended by all students from grades 1 to 5 as a moment of togetherness and the official release of P3K students by the school.

The morning memo began with an introduction of eight P3K students dressed in animal-themed costumes. Given that not every class has ever been taught directly by every student, the animal character approach was chosen as an effective way to facilitate interaction and introduce themselves in a fun way to all students.

Next, students were invited to write letters accompanied by warm instrumental music. This activity gave students the opportunity to write letters to P3K students and to themselves. Several letters were read voluntarily by students, and all collected letters were displayed on a Wall Notes installation as documentation and a token of appreciation for their expressions.

Midway through the event, the school held a symbolic farewell for the P3K students. The event began with remarks from the Principal of UPI Cibiru Laboratory Elementary School, Dr. Enok Jubaedah, M.Pd., followed by the presentation of a visual article plaque as a token of gratitude by student representatives to UPI Cibiru Laboratory Elementary School.

To conclude the series of events, all P3K students and fellow students participated in a flash mob. This event culminated in a lively atmosphere of togetherness and a joyful and collaborative farewell.

The Morning Memo marks the end of the P3K students' time together with the UPI Cibiru Laboratory Elementary School. Besides serving as a send-off, the Morning Memo is expected to leave a lasting impression and become a meaningful experience for the school, students, and university students.
