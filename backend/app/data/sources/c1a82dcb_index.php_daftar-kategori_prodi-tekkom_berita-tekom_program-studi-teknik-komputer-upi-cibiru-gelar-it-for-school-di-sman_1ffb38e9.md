The UPI Cibiru Computer Engineering Study Program Holds "IT for School" at SMAN 24 Bandung and SMKN 2 Bandung

Bandung, November 15, 2025 — The Computer Engineering Study Program at the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) Cibiru Campus has once again demonstrated its commitment to expanding technological literacy through the "IT for School" program held at two high schools in Bandung. The program took place at SMAN 24 Bandung on November 12, 2025 , and continued at SMKN 2 Bandung on November 15, 2025 .

The visit to SMAN 24 Bandung was met with great enthusiasm from the 11th and 12th grade students who participated in the interactive workshop session. Meanwhile, activities at SMKN 2 Bandung focused more on technical practice because the students already had a vocational background, allowing participants to directly try out the equipment and applications brought by the UPI Cibiru Computer Engineering team.

With the implementation of "IT for School" at these two schools, the UPI Cibiru Computer Engineering Study Program affirms its role as an educational institution actively bridging the academic world with the needs of technological literacy in the community. Going forward, similar programs are planned to be expanded to more schools in West Java.

Supporting the SDGs Through Technology Education Initiatives

The "IT for School" initiative serves not only as a platform for sharing knowledge but also as a way to strengthen education's contribution to sustainable development. This initiative aligns with several SDGs, including:

SDG 4 – Quality Education: provide access to quality education in technology, expand students' digital skills, and promote equitable technological literacy in secondary education settings.

SDG 9 – Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure: introducing the latest technologies such as IoT and AI, so that students are prepared to participate in the era of digital industry and sustainable innovation.

SDG 17 – Partnerships for the Goals: creating collaboration between universities and schools in building an educational ecosystem that supports technological development.
