UPI Cibiru Campus and Padang State University Establish Academic Cooperation to Strengthen Basic Education

Bandung, October 22, 2025 — The Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) Study Program at the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) Cibiru Campus officially established an academic partnership with the PGSD Program at Padang State University (UNP). The meeting, held at the UPI Cibiru Campus, marked a significant milestone in strengthening inter-institutional collaboration, particularly in the implementation of the Tridharma of Higher Education, which encompasses education, research, community service, and human resource development.

The event was officially opened by Prof. Dr. H. Karim Suryadi, M.Sc., Director of UPI Cibiru Campus. In his remarks, he emphasized the importance of synergy between higher education institutions in building an innovative and sustainable learning ecosystem.

"This collaboration is not just a formal meeting, but a shared commitment to producing excellent, creative, and character-driven teachers. Collaboration like this is a concrete step toward strengthening basic education in Indonesia," said Prof. Karim.

Meanwhile, Prof. Dr. Yanti Fitria, M.Pd., Head of the Department of Elementary School Teacher Education, Padang State University, expressed her appreciation for the warm welcome from UPI Cibiru Campus and emphasized her hope that this cooperation would be the beginning of a long-term collaboration.

"We see UPI Cibiru as a strategic partner with a similar passion for developing basic education. We hope this collaboration will result in joint programs that will impact teachers and students throughout Indonesia," said Prof. Yanti.

The meeting was attended by 20 UNP lecturers and 80 UNP students, as well as 20 lecturers and 30 students from UPI Cibiru, who together discussed and exchanged ideas on strengthening basic education, teacher professional development strategies, and technology-based learning innovations and local wisdom.

The activity concluded with a group photo session and follow-up discussions to design collaborative programs, such as lecturer exchanges, joint research, and reflective practice-based training for prospective elementary school teachers.

The collaboration between UPI Cibiru Campus and UNP is in line with Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) Number 4, namely "Ensuring inclusive and quality education and promoting lifelong learning opportunities for all."

Through this collaboration, both institutions are committed to:

Improving the quality of primary school teacher education,

Encourage collaborative research relevant to the needs of 21st century education,

Developing innovative and character-oriented learning practices, and

Realizing equal access to quality education throughout Indonesia.
