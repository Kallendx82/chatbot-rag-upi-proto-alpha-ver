The Strategic Role of the UPI Cibiru Campus Mothers' Association (IIK) in Supporting Institutional Activities

UPI Cibiru - The Family Mothers Association (IIK) of the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) Cibiru Campus, chaired by Mrs. Neni Herlina, M.Pd., plays a vital role in supporting various campus activities. As an institutional support organization, IIK serves as a platform that integrates the role of families in supporting the development of educational institutions. IIK's existence not only serves as a driving force for social and community activities but also contributes to creating harmony and synergy among the campus community.

The Director of UPI Cibiru Campus, Prof. Dr. Deni Darmawan, S.Pd., M.Si., M.Kom., MCE., emphasized that the existence of IIK is very significant in supporting the institutional vision and mission. "IIK not only supports institutional activities, but is also able to create a family atmosphere that strengthens cohesion in the campus environment," he said. This is reflected in various IIK programs that involve the UPI extended family, such as social, educational, and cultural activities that have a positive impact on the work and learning climate.

The creativity and dedication of the Indonesian Islamic Student Association (IIK) in implementing its programs adds a unique color to campus life. By upholding the values ​​of togetherness and solidarity, IIK contributes to strengthening relationships among members of the UPI Cibiru Campus family. Furthermore, IIK plays an active role in supporting institutional activities, both through moral and material support, and participation in social and humanitarian activities. Going forward, IIK's continued role is expected to further strengthen the synergistic relationship between the family and the institution at UPI Cibiru Campus. (Ibud).
