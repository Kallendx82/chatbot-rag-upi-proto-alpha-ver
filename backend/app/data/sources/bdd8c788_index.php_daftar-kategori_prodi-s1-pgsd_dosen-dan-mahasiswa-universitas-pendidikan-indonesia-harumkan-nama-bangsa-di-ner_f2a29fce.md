Lecturers and Students of the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) Bring Honor to the Nation at NERPS 2026 Tokyo

TOKYO, JAPAN – The spirit of cross-generational collaboration in the academic world was once again brought by representatives from Indonesia. Dinie Anggraeni Dewi, M.Pd., MH , a lecturer in the Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) study program, along with Fathiannisa Aurelia Nugraha , a student in the PG PAUD study program at the Indonesian University of Education , officially participated in the international conference, The 5th NERPS Conference 2026 .    

The event, organized by The Network for Education and Research on Peace and Sustainability (NERPS) Hiroshima University in collaboration with the United Nations University (UNU), took place on March 4–7, 2026 at UNU Headquarters, Tokyo.      

Elevating the Role of Technology in Global Peace

This year's conference carries the crucial theme of technological innovation in strengthening multilateralism for peace and sustainability. Amid global challenges such as climate change, armed conflict, and social inequality, Dr. Dinie and Fathiannisa are present to share academic perspectives on how the education sector can adapt to digital advancements.

"Our presence here is a concrete manifestation of our transdisciplinary efforts. We want to highlight how technology, if managed wisely from elementary school and early childhood, can be a bridge to a more peaceful and sustainable future," said Dr. Dinie on the sidelines of the event.

International Agenda and Collaboration

Over four days, delegates followed a packed agenda that included:

·        Research Presentation: Presenting the latest research results to global practitioners and academics. 

·        Expert Roundtables: Discuss directly with international experts on the implementation of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).   

·        Innovative Workshop: Exploring new digital tools that can facilitate cultural exchange and environmental risk monitoring. 

Promoting Digital Cooperation

The participation of Dr. Dinie and Fathiannisa aligns with the UN Secretary-General's call for digital cooperation. In an era where technology can be both a threat and an opportunity, the active involvement of the Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) and Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) lecturers and students demonstrates that the foundations of peace must be built early through a technology-savvy yet humanitarian-based education system.

The NERPS 2026 conference is expected to not only be a forum for exchanging ideas, but also a starting point for transdisciplinary research partnerships between educational institutions in Indonesia and global research networks to achieve a greener and more harmonious future.
