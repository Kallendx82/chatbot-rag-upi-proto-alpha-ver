UPI Cibiru Holds ARJUNA Disaster Mitigation Educational Game Workshop in Pasir Jambu District, Bandung Regency.

BANDUNG - The Indonesian Education University (UPI) Cibiru Campus collaborated with the Regional Disaster Management Agency (BPBD) of Bandung Regency to hold an innovative workshop entitled "Implementation of Serious Game-Based Educational Games for Disability-Friendly Disaster Mitigation in Bandung Regency" on Monday (15/9/2025) at the Pasir Jambu District Hall.

This activity, funded by the Directorate of Innovation, Downstreaming, and Science Techno Park, is part of the Community Service (PkM) Innovation Downstreaming Program. All elementary school teachers in Pasir Jambu District participated in the workshop as an effort to strengthen educator capacity in disaster mitigation.

The head of the implementation team, Raditya Muhammad, ST, MT, explained that this program is a collaboration between the UPI higher education institution and the Bandung Regency government which has been ongoing since 2023. As a university in the field of education, the focus of research and community service is carried out consistently involving local government partners and teachers as agents of disaster knowledge transformation in schools "We focus on elementary school students because they are considered to be able to provide an interesting initial knowledge experience, children of this age find it easier to understand something through direct practice and games, ARJUNA (Disaster Sign Application) is here to help teachers and parents provide disaster education in a more fun and interactive way, so that the educational game approach will be more effective in conveying disaster knowledge," he said.

In the workshop, teachers were not only introduced to serious game-based educational games, but also taught disaster prevention and preparedness materials, disaster emergency response efforts, and post-disaster rehabilitation and reconstruction that can be applied in their respective schools.

The Head of the Prevention and Preparedness Division of the Bandung Regency Regional Disaster Management Agency (BPBD), Diki Sudrajat, ST, M.Sc., welcomed the initiative. "The role of the pentahelix in innovative disaster management tailored to the age characteristics of elementary school children is crucial, given their vulnerability. We hope this collaboration can continue to realize Disaster-Safe Education Units (SPAB)," said Diki.

Meanwhile, Nia Kania, S.PT., MIL, a representative from Pasir Jambu District, emphasized the program's relevance to the region's conditions. "This perfectly aligns with Pasir Jambu's needs as a disaster-prone area. As agents of education, teachers are the perfect target and are expected to impart this knowledge to elementary school students throughout Pasir Jambu District," she said.

The Chairperson of the Pasir Jambu Branch of PGRI, Cardi Fadriana, M.Pd., also appreciated this activity as a form of refreshment for teachers to have disaster mitigation competencies according to local wisdom.

The implementation team consists of Raditya Muhammad, ST, MT as the chairman, and members Triana Lestari, S.Psi., M.Pd. and Mochamad Iqbal Ardimansyah, ST, M.Kom., which involves students from the Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) Study Program and the Software Engineering Study Program, UPI Campus in Cibiru.

This program is expected to become a model for innovative disaster mitigation education that can be applied in other regions, particularly in creating a resilient generation that is ready to face disasters from an early age.
