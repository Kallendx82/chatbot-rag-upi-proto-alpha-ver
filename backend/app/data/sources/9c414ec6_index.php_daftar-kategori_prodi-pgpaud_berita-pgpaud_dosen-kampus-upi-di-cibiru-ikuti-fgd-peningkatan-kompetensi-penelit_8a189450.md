Lecturers from UPI's Cibiru Campus Participated in a Focus Group Discussion on Research and Publication Competency Improvement with Lecturers from Flinders University.

Cibiru, November 21, 2024 – Lecturers at the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) Campus in Cibiru participated in a Focus Group Discussion (FGD) entitled “Improving Lecturer Competence in Research and Publication” on Thursday, November 21, 2024. This event presented an expert resource person, Dr. Zamzami Zainuddin from Flinders University, Australia, who shared experiences and strategies related to the development of research and academic publications at the international level.

In this FGD, Dr. Zamzami conveyed the importance of improving the quality of lecturers' research as a strategic step to enhance academic and institutional reputation. He also provided insights on how to effectively write scientific articles, select reputable journals, and strategies for navigating the peer review process. "Research and publication competency not only impacts individual careers but also contributes significantly to the development of science," said Dr. Zamzami.

The discussion was productive, with lecturers sharing various questions and experiences. One topic highlighted was how to leverage digital technology to support interdisciplinary research and international collaboration. "Collaboration with global institutions is a great opportunity to produce relevant and impactful research," added Dr. Zamzami.

The Head of the Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru expressed his appreciation for Dr. Zamzami's presence at the event. "This event is a crucial moment for us to continue improving the capacity of our lecturers to produce quality scientific work. We hope this FGD will motivate lecturers to be more active in research and international publications," he said.

This activity is part of the UPI Campus in Cibiru's efforts to support the Independent Learning and Independent Campus (MBKM) program, one of the focuses of which is strengthening lecturers' competencies to produce research relevant to the needs of society and the global academic world.
