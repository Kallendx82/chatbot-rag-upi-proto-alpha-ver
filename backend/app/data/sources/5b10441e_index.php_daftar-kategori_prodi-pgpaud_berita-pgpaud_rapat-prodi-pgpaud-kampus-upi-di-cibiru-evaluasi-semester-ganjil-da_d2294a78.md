UPI Campus PGPAUD Study Program Meeting in Cibiru: Evaluation of Odd Semester and Preparation for Even Semester of 2025/2026 Academic Year

Cibiru, December 9, 2025 — The Early Childhood Education Teacher Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru held an internal meeting to discuss the evaluation of the implementation of lectures for the Odd Semester of the 2025/2026 Academic Year as well as preparing strategies for the Even Semester of 2025/2026. Held in the PGPAUD Study Room, this activity was attended by the Head of the Study Program, the Secretary of the Study Program, lecturers, and education staff.

The meeting opened with a presentation of the results of the odd semester's academic and non-academic evaluations, covering the effectiveness of the lecture process, student attendance, the quality of field practice, and follow-up on various student feedback. The lecturers also reflected on the learning methods used and the readiness of the teaching materials implemented last semester.

In the planning session for the even semester of the 2025/2026 academic year, the Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) Study Program emphasized strengthening the quality of practice-based learning, refining the RPS (School Plan), enhancing digital literacy, and developing supporting activities such as workshops, collaborations with early childhood education institutions, and community service activities. These steps are designed not only for academic improvement but also to support the achievement of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), specifically:

SDG 4 (Quality Education) — through improving the quality of learning, preparing graduates to be professional educators, and strengthening inclusive and child-centered educational practices.

SDG 5 (Gender Equality) — by ensuring equal access and learning opportunities for all students and promoting a safe, inclusive and discrimination-free learning environment.

SDG 17 (Partnerships for the Goals) — through collaboration with PAUD partners, educational institutions, and community organizations in order to strengthen the early childhood education ecosystem.

The Head of the Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) Study Program emphasized that improving educational quality must go hand in hand with contributing to sustainable development goals. Therefore, every academic agenda is geared towards being more responsive to both global challenges and local needs in the field of Early Childhood Education (PAUD).

The meeting concluded with the development of follow-up actions and the assignment of tasks to the teaching team and educational staff. All meeting participants committed to implementing the formulated strategies, ensuring optimal implementation of the 2025/2026 even semester and aligning with the PGPAUD study program's vision of supporting the achievement of the SDGs through quality early childhood education.
