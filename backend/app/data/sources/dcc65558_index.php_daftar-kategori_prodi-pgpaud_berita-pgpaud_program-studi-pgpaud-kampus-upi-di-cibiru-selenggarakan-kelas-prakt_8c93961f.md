The Early Childhood Education Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru Holds a Practical Class with Koding Next

Cibiru, December 3, 2024 – The Early Childhood Education Teacher Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) Campus in Cibiru held a Practitioner Class activity featuring Dede Nengdiana, S.Pd., a practitioner from Koding Next. This event aims to provide practical insights to students about the application of coding technology for early childhood. As an alumnus of the PGPAUD Study Program at UPI Cibiru Campus, Dede Nengdiana took this opportunity to share her career experiences at Koding Next, a leading technology education institution that focuses on coding learning for children. In her presentation, Dede explained the importance of introducing coding from an early age to develop logical thinking skills, creativity, and 21st-century skills in children.

"Coding for young children isn't just about technology, but also about developing critical thinking and problem-solving skills, which are essential in this digital age," said Dede Nengdiana during the presentation session.

In addition to sharing knowledge, Dede Nengdiana also used this forum to promote flagship programs from Koding Next, specifically designed to support technology-based learning in Early Childhood Education (PAUD). He invited PGPAUD students to explore career opportunities in educational technology and to integrate learning innovations into their future teaching practices. The students in attendance were enthusiastic and actively engaged in discussions, asking various questions about how coding can be applied in the context of early childhood learning. This activity served as an inspiration and added practical insight for prospective educators to adapt to the needs of the times. With the implementation of this Practitioner Class , the PGPAUD Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru once again demonstrated its commitment to bridging students with the professional world, broadening their horizons, and preparing competent graduates to face the challenges of the digital era.
