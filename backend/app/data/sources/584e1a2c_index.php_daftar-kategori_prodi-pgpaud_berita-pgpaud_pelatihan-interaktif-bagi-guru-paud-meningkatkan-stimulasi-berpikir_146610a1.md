Interactive Training for Early Childhood Education Teachers: Enhancing Children's Creative Thinking Stimulation through Project-Based Learning

Cibiru, August 9, 2025 – The Community Service (PkM) Team of the Early Childhood Education Teacher Education Study Program (PGPAUD) at the UPI Cibiru Campus held  an Interactive Training for PAUD Teachers in the Regency and City of Bandung. This activity focused on increasing teacher capacity in implementing Project-Based Learning (PjBL) to encourage the stimulation of creative thinking skills in early childhood. The community service team consisted of UPI Cibiru PGPAUD lecturers, namely Prof. Dr. Leli Halimah, M.Pd., Dr. Tuti Istianti, M.Pd., Desiani Natalina Muliasari, M.Pd., Ph.D., Ayu Hopiani, M.Pd., Mirawati, M.Pd., along with a team of students. They are committed to providing training that is relevant to the needs of PAUD teachers in the 21st century education era.

The training, held on August 9, 2025, at the UPI Cibiru Campus, included interactive discussions, best practice sharing sessions, and workshops designed to strengthen teachers' competencies in designing and implementing project-based learning. One partner teacher shared her impressions:
"This training truly inspired us to develop project-based activities that can foster children's creative thinking skills from an early age. The materials and guidance provided were very applicable for classroom implementation," she said.

Beyond training, the Community Service Program (PKM) activities also included mentoring for the implementation of project-based learning in early childhood education (PAUD) units. This provided teachers with the opportunity to directly practice their acquired knowledge and receive guidance from the community service team. Through this activity, it is hoped that PAUD teachers in Bandung Regency and City will become more creative and innovative in designing meaningful learning experiences, enabling children to optimally develop their creative thinking skills from an early age.
