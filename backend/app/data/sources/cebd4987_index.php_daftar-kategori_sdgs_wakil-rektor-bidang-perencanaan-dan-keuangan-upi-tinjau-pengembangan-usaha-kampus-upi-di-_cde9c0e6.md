UPI's Vice Chancellor for Planning and Finance Reviews Business Development at the UPI Campus in Cibiru

On Wednesday, April 29, 2026, the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) campus in Cibiru received a working visit from the Vice Rector for Planning and Finance, Prof. Dr. Rudi Susilana, M.Si., to monitor and strengthen campus business development. This visit is part of the university's strategic efforts to encourage the optimization of the potential of existing business units on campus to be more productive, sustainable, and provide real benefits to the academic community.

During the visit, Prof. Dr. Rudi Susilana personally inspected several areas with potential for development as units supporting campus independence, one of which was the campus cafeteria. The cafeteria is viewed not only as a facility to support student and staff consumption needs, but also as a vital component in creating a healthy, comfortable, and productive campus ecosystem.

He emphasized that campus canteen development needs to be planned, professional, and focused on excellent service. In addition to cleanliness, comfort, and orderliness, the canteen must also serve as a social interaction space that supports student academic activities. With proper management, the canteen can become a source of campus revenue while providing added value to the entire campus community.

The discussion also covered the importance of structuring campus business management systems to be more transparent, accountable, and sustainable. Business unit development should not focus solely on economic aspects but also consider the principles of environmental sustainability and empowering surrounding communities.

This activity aligns with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), specifically SDG 4 (Quality Education), SDG 8 (Decent Work and Economic Growth), SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Human Settlements), and SDG 12 (Responsible Consumption and Production). Through the development of well-organized campus business units, UPI in Cibiru strives to provide an educational environment that is not only academically superior, but also economically independent and sustainable in its management.

The UPI campus in Cibiru welcomed this working visit as a demonstration of the university leadership's attention and support for strengthening regional campus governance. It is hoped that the results of this visit will serve as a concrete first step toward realizing more optimal, innovative campus business development that positively impacts the entire academic community and the surrounding community. Cbr. 04/29/2026.
