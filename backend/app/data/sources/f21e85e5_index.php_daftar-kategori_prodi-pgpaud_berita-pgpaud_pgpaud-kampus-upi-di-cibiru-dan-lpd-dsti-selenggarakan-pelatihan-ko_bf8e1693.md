UPI's PGPAUD Campus in Cibiru and LPD DSTI Hold Coding and Artificial Intelligence Training for PAUD Teachers in Sumedang Regency

Cibiru, October 31, 2025 — The Early Childhood Education Teacher Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru is once again collaborating with the UPI Digital Development and Information Technology Systems Institute (LPD DSTI) to hold Coding and Artificial Intelligence (AI) Training for PAUD teachers in Sumedang Regency. The activity took place at the UPI Cibiru Campus and was enthusiastically attended by educators who want to improve digital competency in early childhood learning.

This training is designed to introduce basic coding concepts and the use of artificial intelligence technology, which can be adapted for creative and innovative learning. Participants gain an understanding of simple coding activities for young children, the use of educational applications, and strategies for using technology wisely and safely.

The Head of the Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at UPI Cibiru Campus stated that strengthening digital literacy for early childhood education (PAUD) teachers is crucial in today's era of technological transformation. "PAUD teachers must be prepared for technological developments to deliver relevant, enjoyable, and adaptive learning that meets the needs of Generation Alpha," he said.

The LPD DSTI representative also added that this training is part of UPI's commitment to expanding the impact of digital literacy, particularly for educators. This ongoing collaboration is expected to strengthen a technology-friendly learning ecosystem without diminishing the important pedagogical values ​​of early childhood education.

This activity contributes to the achievement of several Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), including:

SDG 4: Quality Education, through increasing teacher capacity in utilizing technology to support quality learning for early childhood.

SDG 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure, by introducing digital technology and artificial intelligence as part of educational innovation.

SDG 17: Partnerships for the Goals, because this activity was implemented through collaboration between PGPAUD UPI Cibiru Campus and LPD DSTI as a form of strategic partnership to improve teacher competency.

The training proceeded smoothly and interactively. The participating PAUD teachers expressed their hope that programs like this could continue regularly as an effort to improve educator professionalism in the digital age.
