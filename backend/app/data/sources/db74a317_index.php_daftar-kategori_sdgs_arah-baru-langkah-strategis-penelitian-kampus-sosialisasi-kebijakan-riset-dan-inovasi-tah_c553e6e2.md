New Directions & Strategic Steps for Campus Research: Socialization of the 2026 Research and Innovation Policy

UPI Cibiru Campus continues to strengthen its academic culture through the 2026 Research and Innovation Policy Socialization event held on the first floor of Building E. The event was attended by campus leaders, heads and members of research groups, research grant winners, and research grant proposers as part of an effort to align the direction of a more measurable and impactful research policy. This event aims to provide a comprehensive understanding of the 2026 research priorities, grant schemes, research governance, and strategies for improving campus publication output and innovation.

Furthermore, this activity strengthens the position of study programs and research groups as drivers of academic innovation. The impact is not only on the number of grant proposals but also on the quality of research, making it more applicable, collaborative, and relevant to the needs of the educational sector and society. Through this activity, UPI Cibiru Campus affirms its commitment to promoting valuable research.

This also relates to SDG 4 (Quality Education) through strengthening the quality of educational research, SDG 9 (Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure) through developing campus innovation, and SDG 17 (Partnerships to Achieve the Goals) through strengthening collaboration between research groups and external partners. Thus, this activity is a real contribution from the campus to building a superior research ecosystem.
