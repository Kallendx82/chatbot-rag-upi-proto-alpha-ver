UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education Lecturers Attend Working Meeting to Review Revision of Main Textbooks for 2025

West Jakarta — Lecturers from the Elementary School Teacher Education Study Program (PGSD) UPI Cibiru Campus also attended and actively participated in the Phase II Review Work Meeting for the Main Textbook Revision held in the context of compiling the 2025 Education Book.

This activity was held at Jl. Hayam Wuruk No. 125, RT. 5/RW. 6, Mangga Besar, Taman Sari District, West Jakarta City, DKI Jakarta 11180, and was attended by educators from various regions. The book review focused on grades III, VI, IX, and XII, with the aim of refining teaching materials to better suit curriculum needs and current developments.

In this activity, Ms. Syifa Nailul Muna Aljamaliah, M.Pd., a lecturer at UPI Cibiru's Elementary School Teacher Education Department, participated as one of the authors. Her presence represents the active contribution of academics in ensuring the quality and usefulness of teaching materials at all levels of elementary and secondary education.

This revision of the main textbook emphasizes not only the informative presentation of the material but also the application of a deep learning approach to teaching. This approach encourages students to deeply understand concepts, develop critical thinking, and develop continuous reflective skills. Therefore, the review process was directed at ensuring that the textbook facilitates an active, meaningful, and intellectually challenging learning experience.

The involvement of UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) lecturers in this activity is part of their commitment to improving the quality of national education. This participation not only contributes to the development of quality learning resources but also strengthens efforts to provide learning that encourages in-depth understanding, not just memorization.

Through this activity, it is hoped that the resulting main textbook can become an important foundation in creating a learning process that is not only informative, but also transformative—encouraging students to think critically, solve problems, and build holistic meaning in every material studied.
