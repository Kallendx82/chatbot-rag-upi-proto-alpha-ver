Elementary School Teacher Education Student Association Holds Book Party 2025

Book Party is a new work program from the Department of Education HIMA PGSD 2025 Grahaabyakta cabinet UPI Cibiru Campus. This activity aims to improve the culture of literacy among PGSD students, especially in terms of reading, critical thinking, and scientific and reflective writing skills. In the instant digital era, Book Party is present as a forum to foster a spirit of in-depth reading and meaningful discussions. Book Party was held for two days, namely on May 15 and 16, 2025 with two complementary activity concepts. The first day of the activity was held in the hallway of the As-Sakinah Mosque, UPI Cibiru Campus. With a beautiful and comfortable open atmosphere, the activity began with a silent reading session , where all participants were asked to read their chosen book independently. This session is a form of personal reflection that provides space for participants to absorb the content of the reading according to their respective styles.

After reading, the activity continued with a sharing session or open discussion. Several participants voluntarily shared the content, messages, and experiences they gained from the books they had read. This aimed to cultivate public speaking skills and open up opportunities for sharing insights among students. The session then evolved into a Forum Group Discussion (FGD), where participants were divided into pro and con groups to discuss an issue or idea relevant to the world of education. This discussion not only trained critical thinking skills but also developed the ability to present arguments in a structured manner, respect others' opinions, and summarize the discussion results collectively. The first day's activities concluded with an internal reflection and evaluation by the committee to ensure the event went according to plan.

On the second day, the Book Party was held at the 3rd Floor of the Bumi Siliwangi Auditorium. This event was more academic in nature, presenting a resource person, Mrs. Cucu Jauriah, S.Pd.I., S.Pd., an author of a collection of mini-fiction books entitled "Suatu Sore di Café" . The material presented consisted of two main parts: book review and writing training. The presentation of the material took place in a relaxed yet insightful atmosphere.

In the book review session, the resource person encouraged participants to deeply understand the structure of the book, from theme, plot, setting, to style. Critical reading and annotation techniques were also discussed, ensuring participants were not merely passive readers but were able to explore meaning and form personal, reflective opinions about their reading. Furthermore, participants were taught how to systematically compose a book review, enabling them to express their understanding in a structured written form.

The next session focused on writing skills. The speakers explained various types of writing and their characteristics, including fiction and nonfiction. The event concluded with an interactive and enthusiastic question and answer session from the participants, demonstrating that literacy and writing are indeed highly relevant and needed topics among PGSD students. The closing ceremony included the presentation of certificates to the speakers and a joint documentation session facilitated by the Publication and Documentation Division (PDD) of the PGSD Student Association.

Book Party 2025 is not just a reading and writing activity, but also a step towards building an active and literacy-conscious academic community. This activity successfully created a safe space for students to share ideas, deepen their thinking, and strengthen solidarity as future educators. Through this activity, it is hoped that UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) students will be able to become pioneers in instilling a culture of sustainable literacy, both on campus and in the future when they enter the world of elementary education.
