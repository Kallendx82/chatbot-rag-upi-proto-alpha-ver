UPI Cibiru Campus Welcomes Associate Professor Takamichi Asakura from Hiroshima University, Japan

The Cibiru campus of the Indonesian University of Education (UIPI) received an academic visit from Associate Professor Takamichi Asakura, Ph.D., from Hiroshima University, Japan, as part of the Adjunct Professor program and the strengthening of international cooperation in the field of education.

The visit was welcomed by the leadership and lecturers of UPI's Cibiru Campus through a warm and friendly gathering and academic discussion. The event also featured an exchange of souvenirs as a symbol of friendship, appreciation, and shared commitment to strengthening the academic network between the two institutions.

The presence of Prof. Takamichi Asakura marks a significant milestone for UPI Cibiru Campus in expanding its global perspective in education, particularly elementary school teacher education. Through various academic programs, it is hoped that a collaborative space will be created that will foster the development of internationally competitive education, research, and community service.

The Adjunct Professor Program is part of UPI's Cibiru Campus's efforts to bring a global perspective to the academic environment and strengthen the university's internationalization. By collaborating with teachers from various countries, the academic community is expected to gain a richer learning experience that is more relevant to developments in global education.

The activity closed with a joint documentation session as a form of appreciation for the good relationship and academic cooperation that continues to develop between UPI Cibiru Campus and Hiroshima University, Japan.
