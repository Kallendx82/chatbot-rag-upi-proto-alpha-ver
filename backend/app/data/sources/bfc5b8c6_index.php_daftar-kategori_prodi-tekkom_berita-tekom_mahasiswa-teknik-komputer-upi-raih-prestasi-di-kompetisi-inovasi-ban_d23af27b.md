UPI Computer Engineering Students Achieve Excellence in the 2025 Bandung Bedas Innovation Competition

Two student teams from the Computer Engineering Study Program of the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) have successfully achieved proud achievements at the 2025 Bandung Bedas Innovation Competition (KIBB), organized by the Regional Development Planning, Research, and Innovation Agency (Bapperida) of Bandung Regency.

This prestigious event, themed "From Vision to Action: Be the Next Innovator," provides a platform for students, pupils, regional officials, and the general public to showcase ideas and innovations that have the potential to support regional development based on creativity and technology.

At the peak of the awards ceremony held at the Grand Sunshine Resort & Convention, Soreang – Bandung Regency, two teams from UPI Computer Engineering succeeded in breaking into the national top ranks:

🥇 Mother's Prayer Team – Top 5 Student Category

With their work, "Pandu-PD (Analysis and Support Platform for Parkinson's Disease)," the Doa Ibu team was named one of the top five innovators in the student category. This innovation presents a technology-based platform that supports early detection and analysis of Parkinson's patients' conditions through data and artificial intelligence approaches. It is hoped that it will assist medical personnel and improve the quality of life for sufferers.

🏅 Velora Team – Top 10 Student Category

The Velora team, with its innovation "BASMI (Clean Bribery Action, Eliminate Discipline)," also achieved a top 10 finish in the student category. BASMI is a technology-based solution designed to promote transparency, integrity, and anti-bribery monitoring within organizations and educational institutions, in line with the clean and innovative spirit of Bandung Bedas .

These two teams demonstrate that UPI Computer Engineering students not only excel academically, but also possess social awareness and the ability to create innovations that have a real impact on society.

"This achievement is proof that UPI Computer Engineering students are able to contribute to the development of regional innovation, while also strengthening the campus' role in building a research and technology ecosystem that benefits the community,"
said one of the accompanying lecturers after the event.

The 2025 KIBB Awarding event was attended by representatives of the Bandung Regency Bapperida, judges from academic and government circles, and all finalists from various categories — from schoolchildren, university students, to regional officials.

Congratulations to the Mother's Prayer Team and the Velora Team on their outstanding achievements! May this achievement inspire other students to continue innovating, creating, and contributing to the advancement of their region and nation.

The news has been linked to the SDGs:

·         SDG 4 – Quality Education

The team's innovation delivers technology that supports digital literacy and more equitable access to learning. With its easy-to-implement approach, this work has the potential to become an educational tool for communities that previously lacked access to technology.

·         SDG 9 – Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure

The projects developed highlight students' abilities in designing efficient and applicable modern computing-based systems. This demonstrates the university's tangible contribution to supporting the regional innovation ecosystem.
