Participation of Lecturers and Students of PGSD UPI Cibiru in KILAU UPI

A total of 15 lecturers from the Elementary School Teacher Education Study Program (PGSD) at the UPI Cibiru Campus participated in the Advanced Information Literacy Class (KILAU) activity which was held on Monday, April 21, 2025. The KILAI activity is a collaborative activity between the UPI Library and the UPI Cibiru Campus as a continuation of the basic literacy class that was previously implemented. The KILAU program aims to improve information literacy skills, especially for lecturers and students.

The event was opened by the Deputy Director of Resources, Finance, and General Affairs at UPI, Dr. Jenuri, S.Ag., M.Pd. In his remarks, he expressed his appreciation to the lecturers who had made time to attend and urged them to maximize the event. "The enthusiasm of the lecturers who attended was extraordinary. This event, God willing, will be beneficial, especially in terms of research publications," he said.

In her remarks, the Head of the Library Services Division of the UPI Library, Santi Santika, MP, emphasized the importance of advanced information literacy for students and lecturers. "We are committed to facilitating lecturers and students in producing quality scientific work. Therefore, the KILAU program is one effort to maintain the quality of scientific work and academic integrity in this challenging digital era," she said.

Meanwhile, 25 students from the Elementary School Teacher Education Program (PGSD) also participated in the activity. They are final-year students currently working on their theses. Their participation is expected to assist them in completing their academic work (theses), particularly in improving their understanding of how to access and utilize the various electronic facilities subscribed to by the Library.
