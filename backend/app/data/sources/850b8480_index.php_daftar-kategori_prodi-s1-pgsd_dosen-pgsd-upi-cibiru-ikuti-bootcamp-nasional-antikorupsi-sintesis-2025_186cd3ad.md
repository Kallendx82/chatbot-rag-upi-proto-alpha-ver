UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education Lecturers Participate in the 2025 National Anti-Corruption Bootcamp

Dr. Agil Nanggala, M.Pd., a lecturer in the Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) Study Program at the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) Cibiru Campus, participated in the National Anti-Corruption Bootcamp: Indonesian Youth Integrity Synergy (SINTESIS) 2025. This activity was attended by hundreds of selected young participants from various regions in Indonesia and was carried out as a form of commitment to instilling integrity values ​​​​from an early age.

The SINTESIS 2025 Bootcamp features a series of activities focused on anti-corruption education, strengthening integrity values, and developing the leadership capacity of the younger generation. Participants receive direct training from experts, practitioners, and national figures concerned with corruption eradication issues.

Dr. Agil expressed his profound impressions of the event. "This bootcamp provides an important opportunity to reflect on the role of educators in instilling integrity in the younger generation. I feel inspired to continue contributing through education, ensuring that the values ​​of honesty, responsibility, and social awareness are truly ingrained in students," he said.

The involvement of UPI Cibiru's Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) lecturers in this activity also demonstrates the university's support for national efforts to build an anti-corruption culture. It is hoped that the experiences and insights gained can be applied in classroom learning and community service activities, thus making a real impact on creating a clean and integrated generation of Indonesians.
