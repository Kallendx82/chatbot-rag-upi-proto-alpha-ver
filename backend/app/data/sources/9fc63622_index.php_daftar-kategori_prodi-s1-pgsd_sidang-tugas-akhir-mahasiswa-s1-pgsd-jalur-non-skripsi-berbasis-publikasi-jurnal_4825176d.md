UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education Undergraduate Students Conduct Non-Thesis Final Projects Based on Deliberative Learning

The Elementary School Teacher Education Study Program (S1 PGSD) of the Indonesian University of Education, Cibiru Campus, once again held a Non-Thesis Final Project as part of fulfilling student learning outcomes. One of the students, Alya Rahmadiyani, has completed a non-thesis final project entitled "Deliberative Learning in Elementary Civic Education: Strengthen Students' Communication Skills from a Vocational Perspective (Strengthening Elementary School Students' Communication Skills Through Deliberative Learning in a Vocational Civic Perspective)".

This final project examines the application of deliberative learning or deliberation in Pancasila and Citizenship Education (PKI) in elementary schools. Through this approach, students are trained to develop communication skills, critical thinking, and democratic attitudes through discussions, expressing opinions, and making decisions collectively. The vocational civic perspective used places communication skills as a crucial competency that needs to be developed starting in elementary school as a foundation for students' future social and academic lives.

The implementation of this non-thesis final assignment is in line with Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) point 4, namely Quality Education, which emphasizes the importance of inclusive, participatory learning, and oriented towards strengthening 21st-century skills. In addition, the deliberative approach in learning also supports SDGs point 16, namely Peace, Justice, and Resilient Institutions, by instilling the values ​​of democracy, tolerance, and responsibility from an early age.

Through the implementation of this non-thesis final assignment, the UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education Undergraduate Study Program continues to be committed to producing prospective elementary school teachers who are professional, innovative, and have character, and are able to contribute to realizing quality and sustainable elementary education.
