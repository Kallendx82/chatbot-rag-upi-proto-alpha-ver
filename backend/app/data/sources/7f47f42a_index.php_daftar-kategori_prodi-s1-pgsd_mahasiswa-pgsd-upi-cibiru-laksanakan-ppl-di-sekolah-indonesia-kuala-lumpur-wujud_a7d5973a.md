UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education Students Conduct Field Experience at the Indonesian School in Kuala Lumpur: A Real Manifestation of SDGs 4 (Quality Education) and 17 (Global Partnerships)

Kuala Lumpur, November 4, 2025 — Six students from the Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) Study Program at the Indonesian Education University (UPI) Cibiru Campus have been officially accepted to undertake the Field Experience Practice Program (PPL) at the Indonesian School of Kuala Lumpur (SIKL), Malaysia.
This activity is part of a collaboration between the Indonesian Education University (UPI) and the Indonesian School Abroad (SILN) under the auspices of the Indonesian Embassy (KBRI) Kuala Lumpur, as a concrete form of cross-border educational collaboration oriented towards the quality and professionalism of prospective educators.

The students who participated in this program were:

Daffa Wahid Prasetyo (2202915)

Anisa Nur Asfiyah (2209161)

Abdul Aziz Sidik (2208372)

Firman Achmad Firdaus (2204227)

Intan Afiyah Sukmawan (2202460)

Salsa Nabila (2209662)

Student admissions will be held on Monday, October 27, 2025, at 9:00 a.m. Malaysian time, at the Indonesian School in Kuala Lumpur (SIKL), under the direct coordination of the Indonesian Embassy in Kuala Lumpur.
The event began with remarks from representatives from UPI and SIKL, followed by a student introduction session, a symbolic handover, and an initial orientation about the school environment, work culture, and educational practice activities that will be carried out during the PPL period.

In his remarks, a representative from the Indonesian School of Kuala Lumpur expressed his appreciation for the collaboration with UPI.

"We welcome the arrival of UPI Cibiru students. We believe they will bring new enthusiasm, creativity, and positive educational values ​​to our students. This program is not only about teaching practice, but also about the exchange of values ​​and culture between generations of educators," said SIKL.

Meanwhile, Dr. Dinie Anggraeni Dewi, M.Pd., MH, as Secretary of the PGSD Study Program, UPI Cibiru Campus, emphasized that this program is a strategic step in expanding students' learning experiences in the global realm.

"This international PPL program is part of UPI Cibiru's PGSD commitment to producing prospective teachers who are not only competent in pedagogy, but also possess global insight, strong character, and cross-cultural adaptability. This is a concrete implementation of the Tridharma of Higher Education, oriented towards the SDGs," said Dr. Dinie.

One of the participants, Daffa Wahid Prasetyo, expressed his impressions and hopes after being officially accepted as a PPL participant in Kuala Lumpur.

"This opportunity has been an incredible experience for my friends and me. We've learned about education, culture, and professionalism in an international context. Hopefully, we can make our best contribution and bring good name to UPI on a global level," Daffa said enthusiastically.

This program also reflects UPI's contribution to the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), in particular:

SDGs 4: Quality Education, by improving the competency of prospective teachers to be ready to face global challenges and create an inclusive, innovative, and competitive learning environment.

SDGs 17: Partnerships to Achieve the Goals, through international collaboration between UPI and the Indonesian Embassy in Kuala Lumpur in strengthening cross-country educational networks.

With the spirit of professionalism and nationalism, PGSD UPI Cibiru students are expected to be able to become ambassadors of Indonesian education, who not only teach knowledge, but also instill the values ​​of character, culture, and national spirit in the international arena.
