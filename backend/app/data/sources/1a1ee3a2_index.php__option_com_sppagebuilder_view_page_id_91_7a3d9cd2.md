Graduates of Study Program

The Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at UPI's Cibiru campus is committed to producing professional teachers and lifelong learners who excel not only in early childhood education but also possess additional competencies in service management and entrepreneurship in the field of early childhood education. As of 2024, the study program has graduated 823 students ready to contribute to various educational institutions, community groups, and develop innovations and independent businesses based on early childhood education.

As part of our efforts to improve the quality and relevance of higher education, the Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru consistently conducts Tracer Studies to track the track records of graduates in the workplace and entrepreneurship. This activity serves as the basis for ongoing evaluation of the achievement of graduate profiles and the suitability of the curriculum to field needs. In addition, we also conduct Alumni User Satisfaction Surveys aimed at superiors, partners, and institutions where graduates work, to obtain objective feedback on the performance and competencies of our alumni. As a complement, we feature testimonials from alumni, describing their experiences, career paths, and impressions during their education in the PGPAUD Study Program. 

Tracer Study

To improve graduate quality and improve the curriculum's alignment with workplace needs, the Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru actively supports the Tracer Study conducted by the Indonesian University of Education. The UPI Tracer Study aims to track alumni career paths, determine job waiting periods, determine the relevance of jobs to their fields of study, and obtain input for developing educational programs that are more responsive to the dynamics of the field.

Tracer study can be accessed through the following link: https://tracerstudy.upi.edu/
