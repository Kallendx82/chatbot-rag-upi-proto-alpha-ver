Profile of the Elementary School Teacher Education Undergraduate Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru: Moving, Progressing, for a Better Indonesia

The Elementary School Teacher Education Study Program, at the Cibiru campus of the Indonesian University of Education, serves as a growth platform for aspiring elementary school educators who are professional, adaptable, and globally competitive. Since its founding in 2026, the Elementary School Teacher Education Study Program at UPI Cibiru has consistently maintained its commitment to improving educational quality, as evidenced by its achievement of UNGGUL accreditation from the Independent Accreditation Institute for Education (LAMDIK) and international accreditation recognition from ACQUIN.

Based on the vision of developing elementary education science based on Indonesian pedagogy, modern psychology, and contemporary educational theory, the Elementary School Teacher Education Program at UPI Cibiru focuses not only on mastering theory but also on strengthening students' character and actual competencies. The learning process is designed to develop elementary school teachers who are able to understand their students holistically, think critically, and respond to the dynamics of the times.

Through the implementation of the Outcome-Based Education (OBE) curriculum and the Student-Centered Learning approach, students are equipped with academic skills, practical skills, and preparedness for the world of work. With a total of 150 semester credit units, students can also choose a specialization in Digital Learning and 21st-Century Education to prepare them for the challenges of modern, increasingly technology-driven education.

The Elementary School Teacher Education Program (PGSD) at UPI's Cibiru campus is supported by comprehensive learning facilities, including classrooms and smart classrooms, various scientific and microteaching laboratories, and educational robotics and multimedia laboratories. This conducive academic environment encourages students to actively innovate, research, and develop creative and contextual learning practices.

Beyond the national level, UPI Cibiru's Elementary School Teacher Education Program (PGSD) also offers opportunities for international experiences through student exchange programs, virtual summer schools, international conferences, visiting professors from foreign universities, and field internships abroad. All of these programs are designed to broaden students' global perspectives without abandoning the Indonesian educational identity.

Graduates of the Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) undergraduate program at the UPI campus in Cibiru are prepared to become superior human resources capable of acting as professional educators, researchers, educational entrepreneurs, and elementary education practitioners both nationally and internationally. Equipped with knowledge of science, technology, art, and humanitarian values, PGSD graduates are poised to become agents of change in advancing elementary education in Indonesia.
