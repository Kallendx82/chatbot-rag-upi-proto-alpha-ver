UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education Student Wins National Gold Medal at the Indonesia Medical Olympiad

Another brilliant achievement was achieved by students of the Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) Study Program at the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) in Cibiru. Salwa Nurafiah, a PGSD student, won a national gold medal in the Indonesian Language category at the Indonesia Medical Olympiad (IMO) #6, organized by GYPEM in collaboration with the Student Executive Board of Ciputra University.

The two-day competition, held in Jakarta from November 1–2, 2025, featured participants from various universities across Indonesia. In the Indonesian Language category, Salwa excelled, demonstrating linguistic analysis, literacy skills, and language accuracy, which the panel of judges deemed the most comprehensive.

The study program expressed its appreciation for this national achievement, while emphasizing that Salwa's achievement is proof that PGSD students are not only competent in the field of education, but are also able to participate in interdisciplinary academic competitions.

Salwa herself expressed her gratitude and motivation after winning the gold medal.

"I'm very grateful for this result. This competition has been a valuable experience that has encouraged me to continue learning and to try new things. I hope this achievement will inspire others to not hesitate to compete and develop their potential," said Salwa.

This achievement also strengthens UPI Cibiru's track record as a study program that encourages its students to actively excel at both national and international levels. In addition to bringing honor to the university, this victory also serves as a boost for improving the quality of Indonesian language literacy and education.

The study program emphasized that support for high-achieving students will continue to be increased to open up wider spaces for creativity and competition.

"Continue your enthusiasm to inspire and bring honor to the PGSD Department at UPI Campus in Cibiru!" reads the official statement from the study program.

With this achievement, Salwa Nurafiah not only proves her academic capacity, but also represents PGSD students who are able to compete on the national stage, bringing a spirit of innovation and dedication to the world of education.
