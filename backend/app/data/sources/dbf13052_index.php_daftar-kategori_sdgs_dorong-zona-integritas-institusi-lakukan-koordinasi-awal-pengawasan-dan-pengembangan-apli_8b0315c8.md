To Promote Integrity Zones, Institutions Conduct Initial Coordination for WBS Application Supervision and Development

Wednesday, January 21, 2026

To promote the implementation of the Integrity Zone (ZI) Program, the institution held an initial coordination meeting to strengthen oversight and develop the Whistleblowing System (WBS) application. This activity is a strategic step towards building transparent, accountable, and integrated organizational governance.

The meeting was led by the Deputy Director for Education, Student Affairs, and Quality Assurance, Dr. Eng. Munawir, MT., and attended by several lecturers from the design and implementation team, as well as five students from the UPI Cibiru Campus. The involvement of the academic community reflects a shared commitment to supporting the strengthening of internal oversight and preventing practices that conflict with integrity values.

The meeting activities were held in the Cawalaloka Room, Building A, 2nd Floor. In the meeting, various designs and plans for programs to be implemented were discussed, especially regarding the development of the WBS Application as a safe, easily accessible, and reliable reporting facility.

The meeting also discussed strategic steps that need to be considered in program implementation, ranging from technical aspects and reporting mechanisms to outreach to the entire academic community. It is hoped that this initial coordination will optimize oversight and enable the WBS application to become a key supporting tool in realizing an Integrity Zone within the institution.
