To Strengthen the Competence of Prospective Teachers, the Multimedia Education Study Program Delegates First Aid Students to SMK Pasundan 3 and SMKN 10 Bandung.

BANDUNG – The Multimedia Education Study Program at the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) Cibiru Campus has once again sent its students to participate in the Professional Strengthening Program in Education (P3K). The student drop-off and handover activities were held at two strategic partner schools in Bandung: SMK Pasundan 3 Bandung and SMKN 10 Bandung .

This activity is an initial step for final year students to experience real-life experiences directly in a school environment, both in terms of school management aspects and teaching practice in the classroom.

Accompanied by a Field Supervisor

In practice, the student handover process is carried out directly by a Field Supervisor (DPL) appointed by the study program. The presence of the supervisor aims to ensure good initial communication between the university and the partner school.

The two lecturers from the Multimedia Education Study Program who were in charge of this delivery were Intan Permata Sari, S.St., M.Ds. and Sri Lestari Harja, S.Pd., MT.

Meanwhile, Intan Permata Sari, S.St., M.Ds., emphasized the importance of adapting to work culture. She advised students to focus not only on the teaching material but also on the values ​​of professionalism and teaching ethics implemented at SMK Pasundan 3 and SMKN 10 Bandung.

Synergy with Leading Vocational Schools

The selection of SMK Pasundan 3 Bandung and SMKN 10 Bandung as the P3K locations was based on their reputations in technology and the creative arts. This was deemed highly aligned with the competencies of UPI Cibiru Multimedia Education students.

The school warmly welcomed the students and their supervising lecturers. The handover ceremony was marked by the signing of minutes and introductions to the Supervising Teachers who will be assisting the students during the P3K period.

Through this program, students are expected to be able to hone the four basic teacher competencies (pedagogical, personality, social, and professional) before officially earning a Bachelor of Education degree.
