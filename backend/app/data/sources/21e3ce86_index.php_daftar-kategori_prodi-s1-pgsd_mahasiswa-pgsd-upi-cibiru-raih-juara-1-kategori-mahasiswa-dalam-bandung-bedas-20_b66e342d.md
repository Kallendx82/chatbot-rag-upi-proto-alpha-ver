UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education Students Win First Place in the Student Category at Bandung Bedas 2025

Two students of the Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) Study Program at the Indonesian Education University (UPI) Cibiru Campus, Anisa Rohmah Hasanah and Indri Salsabila, successfully won 1st place in the Student category in the 2025 Bandung Bedas Innovation Competition. This victory confirms the role of PGSD students as drivers of educational innovation that is relevant to regional development.

Themed "From Vision to Action: Be the Next Innovator ," Bandung Bedas 2025 provided a platform for young talents to showcase their innovative ideas. Under the guidance of Triana Lestari, S.Psi., M.Pd., Anisa and Indri presented work deemed capable of bridging the gap between basic education needs and creative, applicable solutions.

"This isn't just about the title, but rather proof that educational ideas born on campus can have a real impact on schools and communities," said Triana Lestari. "I'm proud to see how they design solutions based on the real needs of teachers and students."

The winners echoed similar sentiments. Anisa explained, "This competition forced us to think pragmatically: how innovative visions can be translated into concrete actions in the classroom. We are grateful for the support of our study program and our supervisors who guided us from concept to prototype." Indri added that the competition process strengthened their collaboration, small-scale research, and scientific presentation skills.

This achievement not only reinforces the prestige of the individual and study program but is also relevant to the global development agenda. The victory aligns with SDG 4 (Quality Education) by promoting quality teacher education and access to innovative learning practices. Furthermore, the innovation and development of technological/pedagogical solutions demonstrated support SDG 9 (Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure), particularly efforts to foster the innovative capacity of the younger generation to strengthen adaptive educational infrastructure.

The Elementary School Teacher Education Program stated that it will continue to encourage students to participate in various competitions and programs that enhance their professional competencies. "This victory motivates us to expand our creativity and innovation literacy development programs for students," said a representative from the study program.

Anisa and Indri's achievements serve as a concrete example of how higher education—through systematic mentoring and a competitive platform—can generate relevant solutions for education. Hopefully, this achievement will spark more creative work from UPI Cibiru's Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) students for the advancement of national education.
