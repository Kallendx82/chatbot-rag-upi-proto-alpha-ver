UPI Cibiru Students' Educational Visit to Commission I of the Indonesian House of Representatives: Learning Firsthand About the Role of Legislative Institutions

The Cibiru campus of the Indonesian Education University ( UPI) continues to strengthen students' national insight and understanding of state administration through academic visits to Commission I of the Indonesian House of Representatives (DPR RI ) . This activity provides students with hands-on learning opportunities to understand the role of legislative institutions in the democratic process and state policy formulation, while also strengthening leadership character and national awareness among the younger generation.

The academic visit and public lecture took place on Wednesday, February 11, 2026, at the Nusantara Building of the Indonesian House of Representatives (DPR RI) in Jakarta. During this activity, students attended a presentation on national insight and the duties and functions of Commission I of the DPR RI, which oversees defense, foreign affairs, communications and informatics, and state intelligence. Students also had the opportunity to engage in direct dialogue with speakers from the DPR RI to gain a deeper understanding of the working mechanisms of the legislative body.

Beyond academic pursuits, this program is part of an effort to provide impactful education for students. Through hands-on learning experiences in state institutions, students are expected to improve their critical thinking and communication skills, as well as their awareness of active participation in democratic life. This activity also aligns with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) , specifically Goal 4 on quality education and Goal 16 on strong and transparent institutions.

As a follow-up, collaboration between universities and state institutions is expected to continue to be strengthened through academic visits, guest lectures, and public policy discussion forums. These activities are expected to provide meaningful learning experiences and foster a sense of nationalism and responsibility in students as the nation's future generation.
