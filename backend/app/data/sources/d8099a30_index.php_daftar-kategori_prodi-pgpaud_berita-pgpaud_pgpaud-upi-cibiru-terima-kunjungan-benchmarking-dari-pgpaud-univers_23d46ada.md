UPI Cibiru PGPAUD Receives Benchmarking Visit from Pelita Bangsa University PGPAUD

Cibiru, December 4, 2025 — The Early Childhood Education Teacher Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru received an official visit from the PGPAUD Study Program at Pelita Bangsa University (UPB) for curriculum benchmarking and external accreditation instruments for LAMDIK. The meeting took place in the PGPAUD Study Program room at UPI Cibiru and took place in a warm, professional, and collaborative atmosphere.

This benchmarking activity aims to strengthen both parties' understanding of best practices in curriculum development, preparation of learning documents, and fulfillment of external accreditation instruments according to LAMDIK standards. The UPB team gathered information related to the development of the CPL, the OBE-based curriculum structure, learning evaluation mechanisms, and the strategies of the UPI Cibiru PGPAUD Study Program in maintaining academic quality and managing the study program.

The Head of the Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at UPI Cibiru, along with lecturers, provided an in-depth presentation on curriculum implementation, internal quality assurance systems, accreditation instrument development, and innovative strategies for strengthening academic services for students. An interactive discussion was also held to share experiences and challenges faced in managing early childhood education (PAUD) study programs in the current digital era.

This activity marks a crucial milestone in strengthening academic networks between universities, particularly in the field of early childhood education. The UPI Cibiru PGPAUD (Early Childhood Education) Department expressed its appreciation for UPB's visit and hopes this collaboration will continue to grow, including research collaborations, joint lectures, and other program developments.

Contribution to SDGs

This benchmarking activity also supports:

SDG 4: Quality Education, through improving the quality of curriculum, academic governance, and accreditation which will impact the quality of future PAUD educator graduates.

SDG 17: Partnerships for the Goals, by building partnerships between universities to support each other in improving the quality of higher education, especially in the field of Early Childhood Education.

Through this activity, PGPAUD UPI Cibiru reaffirmed its commitment to improving the quality of education and expanding its network of collaborations for the sake of continuously strengthening quality.
