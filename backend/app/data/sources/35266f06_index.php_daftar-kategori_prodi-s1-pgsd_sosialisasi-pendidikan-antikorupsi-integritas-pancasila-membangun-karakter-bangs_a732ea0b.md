Socialization of Anti-Corruption Education & Pancasila Integrity Building a Clean and Ethical National Character Emphasizes the Philosophy and Values ​​of Integrity for Students

 

 

The Indonesian University of Education (UPI) Cibiru Campus held an Anti-Corruption Education Socialization event on Wednesday, December 17, 2025, at 8:00 a.m. WIB. The event was held at the Blue Building, Bumi Siliwangi Auditorium, 3rd Floor, and was attended by third-, fifth-, and seventh-semester students. This activity is part of the campus's efforts to instill the values ​​of integrity and build a clean and ethical academic culture.

The anti-corruption education outreach program aims to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the philosophy of anti-corruption, as well as the concepts and practices of anti-corruption in everyday life. This activity encourages students to understand that corruption is not only defined as a large-scale violation of the law, but can also stem from dishonest behavior, abuse of trust, and indiscipline within the academic environment.

The socialization material was delivered by Dr. Edi Kusnadi M.Pd., and Dr. Dinie Anggraeni, M.Pd. , MH. The speakers explained the concept of anti-corruption systematically, starting from the philosophical foundation of anti-corruption, the basic values ​​that underlie it, to the importance of internalizing these values ​​in students' lives. The values ​​of honesty, responsibility, discipline, justice, and integrity were the main points emphasized throughout the activity.

In their presentation, the speakers also emphasized the strategic role of students as agents of change in preventing and rejecting corrupt behavior. Students, particularly as future educators, are expected to be role models in their attitudes and actions, both on campus and in the wider community. Thus, anti-corruption education goes beyond conceptual understanding and is also translated into concrete behavior.

Through this Anti-Corruption Education Socialization activity, UPI Cibiru Campus hopes that students will develop a critical awareness of the dangers of corruption and internalize anti-corruption values ​​as part of their personal and professional character. This activity is expected to be a sustainable step in building an anti-corruption culture on campus and supporting the development of UPI graduates with character, integrity, and a commitment to national values.
