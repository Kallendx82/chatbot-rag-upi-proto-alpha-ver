Three PGPAUD Students Selected as Outstanding Students at the Study Program Level in 2025

Cibiru, February 3, 2025 — The Early Childhood Education Teacher Education (PGPAUD) Study Program of UPI Cibiru Campus has successfully conducted the 2025 Outstanding Student Selection (Pilmapres) for Study Program Level on Monday, February 3, 2025. This activity is part of a series of annual selections aimed at recruiting the best students from various levels, from the study program level to the national level.

In this year's selection, three students were selected as the best representatives from PGPAUD, namely:

Arij Fathi Bauzir (2204035)

Sipa Sopiah (2207702)

Siti Taghsya Mumtazayyanah (2407968)

All three were deemed to have outstanding academic and non-academic achievements, demonstrating dedication and passion for self-development, leadership, and positive contributions to society. They successfully demonstrated their qualities as outstanding and competitive early childhood educators.

The determination of Outstanding Students is not only a competition event, but also plays an important role in supporting the achievement of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), in particular:

SDG 4: Quality Education, because the Pilmapres encourages students to continuously improve their academic quality, pedagogical literacy, and professional skills as prospective educators who will contribute to improving the quality of early childhood education.

SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth, through improving students' competencies and independence so that they are better prepared to face the world of work and create sustainable work opportunities in the field of education.

The Early Childhood Education Program (PGPAUD) at UPI Cibiru Campus extends its congratulations and highest appreciation for the success of these three students. May this achievement serve as a stepping stone to continued growth, innovation, and inspiration for their colleagues, elevating UPI Cibiru Campus's reputation to greater heights, while contributing to the achievement of sustainable, quality education.
