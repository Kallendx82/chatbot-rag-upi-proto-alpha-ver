Happy Proposal Seminar!

Bandung, September 15, 2025 – The UPI Cibiru Campus S1 PGSD Study Program held a Thesis Proposal Seminar for students of the Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) Study Program on Monday and Tuesday, September 15–16, 2025. The activity took place from 08.00 to 16.00 WIB in several rooms, including Building X Library, Journal Room, PGSD S2 Study Room, and Cawalaloka Room.

This thesis proposal seminar activity involved 30 examining lecturers, including Dr. Yeni Yuniarti, M.Pd., Dr. Tita Mulyati, M.Pd., Dr. Yunus Abidin, M.Pd., Dr. Dinie Anggraeni Dewi, S.Pd., M.Pd., MH, Dr. Rendi Restiana Sukardi, M.Pd., and a number of other lecturers.

The participants came from various fields of study, including Science Education, Mathematics, Arts, Social Studies, Language, Civics, and Education. The students' thesis titles were diverse, covering the development of digital learning media, the implementation of innovative learning models such as Problem-Based Learning (PBL), Project-Based Learning (PjBL), and STEM, as well as the integration of local culture into learning.

In his remarks, Prof. Karim Suryadi emphasized the importance of the thesis defense as the final stage of a student's academic process. "The thesis defense is not only a test of research results, but also a way to shape the academic character of prospective teachers, ready to face the challenges of 21st-century education," he said.

This activity is expected to enable students to demonstrate the academic abilities, creativity, and professional competencies they have acquired throughout their studies. This thesis defense also serves as a crucial milestone leading up to the graduation ceremony, which will determine the graduation of Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) students at UPI Cibiru Campus in September 2025.
