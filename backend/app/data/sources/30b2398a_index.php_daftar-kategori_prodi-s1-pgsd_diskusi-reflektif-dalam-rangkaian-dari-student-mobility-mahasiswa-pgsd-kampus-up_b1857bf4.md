Reflective Discussion in the Series of Student Mobility of PGSD Students from UPI Cibiru Campus to the Indonesian School Kuala Lumpur (SIKL) Malaysia

On Monday, August 4, 2025, a reflective discussion was held as part of the Student Mobility program for Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) students to the Indonesian School of Kuala Lumpur (SIKL) and the Indonesian School of Johor Baru (SIJB). The discussion was led by Dr. Dinie Anggraeni Dewi, M.Pd. and attended by six undergraduate PGSD students from UPI Cibiru Campus, namely:

Daffa Wahid Prasetyo (2202915)
Anisa Nur Asfiyah (2209161)
Abdul Aziz Sidik (2208372)
Firman Achmad Firdaus (2204227)
Intan Afiyah Sukmawan (2202460)
Salsa Nabila (2209662)

The focus of this activity was the exchange of insights and pedagogical practices across borders in Indonesian schools abroad. The discussion covered several important aspects, such as the division of student roles during fieldwork, understanding the curriculum used in SIKL and SIJB, and the adaptive approach needed to address the cultural diversity of students there.

This activity is also a form of real contribution to the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), in particular:

SDG 4 ( Quality Education ), because students are directly involved in efforts to improve the quality of education across countries, as well as broaden their pedagogical insights through practice in different contexts.
SDG 17 ( Partnership for the Goals ), because this program reflects international cooperation between educational institutions in Indonesia and Malaysia in supporting inclusive and sustainable quality education.

As a result of the discussion, it was agreed that students would be divided into two teams to undertake field experience programs at SIKL and SIJB. The focus of observations would be on classroom management strategies, multicultural approaches to learning, and school community participation. Each student was required to prepare a daily reflection report. Coordination was carried out with the supervising lecturer and the local school administration.

To strengthen documentation of activities and disseminate learning outcomes, a video documentation of the entire series of activities is also planned. This activity is a crucial first step in preparing students professionally and academically for international programs aligned with the vision of global education.
