The UPI Cibiru Campus's PKN PGSD KBK Holds a National Seminar on Pancasila Education

In an effort to improve the understanding and application of Pancasila values ​​and citizenship among the younger generation. On Friday, November 7, 2025, the Lecturer Team of the Civics Education Expertise Group (KBK PKn) of the Elementary School Teacher Education Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru held a national webinar on Civics Education (PKn) in 2025 in collaboration with two renowned universities, namely Yogyakarta State University and Tadulako University, which was held online via Zoom meeting.

This National Civics Webinar carried the theme "Transformation of Pancasila and Citizenship Education in the Disruptive Era: Challenges and Innovations in Supporting Sustainable Development" which was attended by more than 700 participants from various circles, including students, lecturers, and education practitioners, presenting six inspiring speakers who are competent in their fields, including:

1.     Dr. Dinie Anggraeni Dewi, M.Pd., MH from the Indonesian University of Education;

2.     Dr. Agil Nanggala, M.Pd. from the Indonesian University of Education;

3.     Dr. Yayuk Hidayah, M.Pd. from Yogyakarta State University;

4.     Dr. Iqbal Arpannudin, M.Pd. from Yogyakarta State University;

5.     Asep Anggi Buldani, M.Pd., MH from Tadulako University;

6.     Sukmawati, M.Pd. Tadulako University.

This National Civics Webinar was held for the first time as a forum for academics, education practitioners, students, and civics observers to discuss, share ideas, and formulate innovative strategies in developing Civics learning in the digital era.

This activity is expected to generate insights and recommendations that can strengthen the role of Pancasila and Civics Education in shaping a young generation with character, critical thinking, adaptability, and a global perspective, while remaining rooted in Pancasila values. With this webinar, the transformation of civics education will not only be a response to the challenges of the disruptive era but also a tangible contribution to supporting sustainable national development.

The event concluded with the hope that the values ​​of Pancasila would remain alive and relevant among the younger generation and serve as a foundation for building a just and sustainable society. Participants were expected to apply the discussions and knowledge gained in their daily lives and in the teaching and learning process at their respective institutions.
