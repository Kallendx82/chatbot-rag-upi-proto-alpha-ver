UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education Student Wins Bronze Medal at National Airlangga Competition 11

Cibiru, October 2025 — Another proud achievement was achieved by a student of the Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) Study Program at the Indonesian Education University (UPI) Campus in Cibiru. Pebi Pebriyanti, a third-semester PGSD student, won a Bronze Medal in the Indonesian Language category at the Airlangga Competition 11, held by PT. Lentera Pendidikan Indonesia at the national level.

This prestigious competition provides an opportunity for students from various universities across Indonesia to express their academic abilities and language literacy creatively and scientifically. In the competition, Pebi demonstrated exceptional writing skills and communicated reflective and innovative linguistic ideas, earning her a top three place nationally.

This achievement also demonstrates the dedication of UPI Cibiru's Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) students in developing academic competency, critical thinking skills, and linguistic literacy. This achievement not only reflects individual excellence but also demonstrates the program's success in nurturing students to compete effectively at the national level.

In her short interview, Pebi Pebriyanti expressed her gratitude and hopes:

"I feel very grateful to be able to represent the UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education Department in this national event. This competition isn't just about winning, but also about learning and the courage to step out of your comfort zone. I hope this experience will motivate others to continue to try and develop their potential."

This achievement aligns with Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) Number 4, namely "Quality Education ." Through active student participation in national academic events, UPI Cibiru contributes to creating an inclusive, equitable, and globally competitive education system.

The UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) Study Program remains committed to encouraging high-achieving students through various self-development programs, scientific competitions, and literacy-based activities. This is expected to produce future teachers who excel not only in pedagogical competence but also possess character, integrity, and the spirit of lifelong learning.

"Achievements like these are proof that UPI Cibiru's Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) students are making a real contribution to the university's reputation. Hopefully, more students will be motivated to excel and become role models in the world of education," said Dr. Dinie Anggraeni Dewi, M.Pd., secretary of the UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) study program.

With the spirit of “Serving for Quality Education” , Pebi Pebriyanti has shown that PGSD students not only play a role as future teacher candidates, but also as agents of change who bring the values ​​of literacy, culture, and intellectuality to a more advanced Indonesia.
