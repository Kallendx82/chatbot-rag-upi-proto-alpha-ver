Staff and Lecturers of the Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru Participate in IT Specialist Competency Certification Training and Exams

Cibiru, November 14, 2024 – Staff and lecturers of the Early Childhood Education Teacher Education Study Program (PGPAUD) at the UPI Campus in Cibiru have successfully participated in the IT Specialist Competency Training and Certification Exam held on November 13-14, 2024. This activity aims to increase the capacity of teaching and education staff in the field of information technology to support a more innovative and efficient learning process.

This training covers a variety of IT skills, such as data management, productivity software, and mastery of digital learning support applications. All participants also take a certification exam at the end of the program to measure their competency in the areas they have learned.

The Head of the PGPAUD Study Program stated that this training aligns with the study program's commitment to supporting digital transformation in education. "Technological advancements require us to continuously learn and adapt. With this certification, staff and lecturers not only strengthen their individual competencies but also contribute to improving the quality of educational services at PGPAUD," he said.

Through this activity, the Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at UPI's Cibiru campus demonstrates its commitment to producing superior educators who are ready to face the challenges of 21st-century education. "With our mastery of technology, we are confident we can provide the best learning experience for students and produce highly competitive PAUD teacher candidates," the statement concluded.

The PGPAUD Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru Conveys Happy Heroes' Day Greetings

Cibiru, November 10, 2024 – In commemoration of National Heroes Day , the Early Childhood Education Teacher Education Study Program (PGPAUD) of the UPI Campus in Cibiru expressed its respects to all the nation's heroes who have fought for Indonesia's independence and sovereignty.

Heroes' Day, celebrated every November 10th, holds profound significance in the nation's history. This date was chosen to commemorate the Battle of Surabaya in 1945, in which thousands of fighters died defending independence from Allied forces. The courage and fighting spirit of the people of Surabaya, led by figures such as Bung Tomo, symbolized the Indonesian nation's resilience in resisting colonialism.

In a statement, the Head of the Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru emphasized the importance of instilling the spirit of Heroes' Day in education. "Heroes' Day is not just a moment to commemorate the services of heroes, but also an opportune time for us as educators to instill the values ​​of struggle, sincerity, and devotion to the nation in the younger generation, including early childhood," he said.

Although there are no special celebrations held within the Study Program environment, the entire academic community is encouraged to remember the services of heroes in their own ways, whether through reflection, prayer, or other positive activities.

The PGPAUD Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru Holds a Visiting Professorship with Lecturers from the University of Malaya1. The PGPAUD Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru Holds a Visiting Professorship with Lecturers from the University of Malaya

Cibiru, November 7, 2024 – The Early Childhood Education Teacher Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru successfully held a Visiting Professor activity on the PAUD Curriculum course, which took place from November 4 to 7, 2024. This activity presented a special resource person, namely Associate Prof. Dr. Mohd Nazri Bin Abdul Rahman from Universiti Malaya, Malaysia, who is known as an expert in the field of curriculum and competency development of PAUD teachers in the 21st century.

Over four days, Dr. Mohd Nazri provided in-depth insights into the Malaysian early childhood education curriculum and the professional competencies needed for early childhood education teachers to meet the challenges of 21st-century education. In one session, he highlighted the importance of adapting the curriculum to meet children's needs and strategies for developing teacher capacity to address global change.

Students and lecturers at the Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) program at UPI's Cibiru campus enthusiastically participated in each session, which included not only lectures but also interactive discussions. "We are very grateful for this opportunity, as it provides an international perspective on the management and development of the Early Childhood Education (PAUD) curriculum," said the Head of the PGPAUD Study Program, who also hopes the collaboration with Universiti Malaya will continue into other programs.

This Visiting Professor activity is one of the efforts of the PGPAUD UPI Campus in Cibiru in implementing the Independent Learning Campus (MBKM) program by presenting international collaborations, while providing cross-cultural learning experiences to students.
