The Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru Holds a Visiting Professorship with Lecturers from Flinders University

Cibiru, November 22, 2024 – The Early Childhood Education Teacher Education Study Program (PGPAUD) at the UPI Campus in Cibiru successfully held a Visiting Professor activity for the ICT Literacy and Learning Media course, which took place from November 18 to 21, 2024. This activity presented an international resource person, Dr. Zamzami Zainuddin from Flinders University, Australia, an expert in the field of educational technology and learning media development in the digital era.

In interactive sessions, Dr. Zamzami presented material related to the use of digital technology-based learning media for early childhood education teachers. He emphasized the importance of innovation in designing media that suits the characteristics of early childhood, while also leveraging technology to create interactive and meaningful learning experiences.

Dr. Zamzami also shared practical examples of technology applications in learning, such as the use of educational apps, augmented reality (AR), and blended learning strategies that can be adapted by early childhood education (PAUD) teachers. "PAUD teachers are not only required to be creative but also need to have strong digital literacy to face the challenges of education in this technological era," he emphasized.

This activity was met with great enthusiasm by PGPAUD students and lecturers. The Head of the PGPAUD Study Program expressed his appreciation to Dr. Zamzami for his contribution. "The presence of this international resource person provides new insights for our students and lecturers in utilizing technology to improve the quality of learning in PAUD. We hope this collaboration can continue," said the Head of the Study Program.

This Visiting Professor program is one implementation of the Independent Learning and Independent Campus (MBKM) program, which provides students with access to learning from global experts. It is hoped that this program will prepare PGPAUD students at the UPI Campus in Cibiru to become professional educators who are adaptable to technological developments and educational needs in the 21st century.
