“PAMUNGKAS” (Musical Performance from Used Goods): A Legacy from the P3K Program Students at SDN 019 PABAKI

Bandung, June 16, 2025 – Students of the Professional Education Strengthening Program (P3K) of the Elementary School Teacher Education Study Program (PGSD) of the Indonesian University of Education, Cibiru Campus, successfully held a Seminar entitled “PAMUNGKAS (Musical Performance from Used Goods) . This activity was held at SDN 019 Pabaki Bandung and was attended by 78 participants consisting of the principal, all teachers and school staff, and students. This seminar is part of the P3K program activities initiated by students Simanjuntak Grace Octora (chairperson), Ainun Nurlatifah, Putri Hurun, Reviliani, and Yoga Firmansyah, with guidance from the Field Supervisor, Ranu Sudarmansyah, M. Pd.

This seminar presented Mr. Ranu Sudarmansyah, M. Pd as a speaker in delivering about used goods, their benefits and functions, as well as the results of utilizing used goods that can show the talents of students at SDN 019 Pabaki Bandung. The seminar resulted in performances from 18 students in playing musical instruments from used goods and singing. Students were initially directed to bring some used goods from their respective homes, such as empty bottles containing a little rice, buckets or cans that were no longer used, unused glass bottles, and several spoons. In the first meeting, they were invited to first find out about the activities that would be carried out, determine the song, and were given an understanding of how to recognize beats and make beats from used goods that would be used. In the second meeting, they began to bring the tools they had.

Each practice session only takes an hour. By the third session, they've adapted to their instruments. However, there are occasional obstacles, such as the inability of some members to attend due to illness or other obligations, which can make it difficult for the children to adjust the beats. They are very enthusiastic about performing and showcasing the results of their practice. Although they have to repeat almost every practice session several times due to mis-beats, this doesn't dampen their desire to perform well.

Beyond the children's enthusiasm, the enthusiasm and support from their parents was also immense. Their parents were thrilled when the students performed. The positive response from the parents during the children's rehearsals made them and us all excited throughout the rehearsals. When performing, the students wore traditional pangsi clothes with headbands, and the female students wore kebaya. This seminar had many positive impacts for all parties. The enthusiasm of the students, teachers at SDN 019 Pabaki, and their parents, since the inception of "Pamungkas," has been a key factor in our success in carrying out this activity. The overwhelming support for the students has encouraged them to practice and perform well.

This seminar delivered many valuable lessons. It began with a comprehensive understanding and concrete examples of how recyclable materials can be used for their own benefit, enabling the children to not only understand the concept but also demonstrate it through their own actions. Furthermore, the positive response from teachers, parents, and even Pamungkas students made this event a success. It provided tangible evidence that strong collaboration yields positive results, and as teachers, we were able to see the talents of Pamungkas students that can be developed at the next level.
