Multimedia Education Students from UPI Cibiru Compete in the 2025 KIBB Competition, Showcasing Appropriate Technology Innovation

SOREANG (September 30, 2025) – Students of the Multimedia Education Study Program at the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) in Cibiru have once again demonstrated their innovative spirit. This time, a delegation of multimedia students participated in the prestigious Bandung Bedas Innovation Competition (KIBB) 2025 , organized by the Bandung Regency Government.

The event, which took place on Tuesday, September 30, 2025, in the Soreang area, became a stage for young innovators to showcase creative solutions to solving problems in society.

Under the guidance of expert lecturers

The Multimedia Education student team advanced to this competition under the intensive guidance of senior lecturer, Mr. Agus Juhana, S.Pd., MT.

In this competition, student teams showcased innovative products in the form of [Name of Product/Innovative Work here, e.g.: Tourism Education Application / Agricultural IoT System / Interactive Multimedia] . This work was designed in response to regional development challenges that are a primary focus of the Bandung Bedas vision.

Mr. Agus Juhana, S.Pd., MT, who accompanied the students at the event location, said that this participation was not just about chasing the championship, but also proving the students' competence in the real world.

"We encourage students to not only write their theses on paper, but also to create products that have utility. Through KIBB in Soreang, students learn how their multimedia technology ideas are validated directly by the local government and the general public," said Agus Juhana on the sidelines of the innovation exhibition.

Fierce Competition for Regional Innovators

This year's Bandung Bedas Innovation Competition (KIBB) attracted a wide range of participants, from schoolchildren and university students to the general public from across Bandung Regency. The UPI student exhibition stand in Cibiru was enthusiastically visited by judges and visitors interested in the technology demonstrations on display.

This participation is expected to motivate other students in the Multimedia Education Study Program to continue to work and be sensitive to social issues that can be solved with a multimedia technology approach.

As of this writing, the team was still undergoing judging sessions and final presentations. The entire academic community has high hopes that this team will bring home the top award for the UPI Campus in Cibiru.
