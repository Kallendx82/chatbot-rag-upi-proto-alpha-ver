UPI CIBIRU LECTURER'S SERVICE: LOCAL COMMUNITY EMPOWERMENT THROUGH SPECIALTY COFFEE DEVELOPMENT IN MARGAMUKTI VILLAGE, PANGALENGAN DISTRICT, BANDUNG REGENCY

Bandung Regency, September 29, 2025. A team of lecturers from the Cibiru Campus of the Indonesian University of Education (UIPI) and Elementary School Teacher Education students conducted a Community Service (PkM) activity in Margamukti Village, Bandung Regency. This program focuses on community empowerment through the development of specialty coffee as an effort to improve the local economy while supporting the achievement of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

Margamukti Village was chosen as the location for this community service project due to its fertile soil, which offers high potential for coffee cultivation. However, the village community still faces several obstacles, such as a lack of technical knowledge about coffee processing, a tendency to focus on quantity over quality, and limited infrastructure for specialty coffee cultivation. This has resulted in the coffee produced not meeting the highest quality standards.

The main community service activity in Margamukti Village included a socialization session led by Dr. Agil Nanggala, M.Pd., on civic empowerment, sociotechnology, and the marketing mix in coffee development. This presentation emphasized the importance of active community participation in leveraging local potential, particularly coffee, to strengthen the village's economic competitiveness.

The community response to this activity was very positive. Participants welcomed the new insights it provided. It is hoped that this activity will open up opportunities for the younger generation and village communities to play a role in developing coffee as a leading commodity.

In addition to supporting village potential, this community service also aligns with SDGs 2 (Zero Hunger) , which promotes increased food security through sustainable agriculture, and SDG 8 (Decent Work and Economic Growth) , which focuses on the importance of decent work for inclusive economic growth. Based on these points, this activity plays a role in strengthening community capacity so that they can develop coffee as a source of added economic value through better processing, branding, and marketing, so that coffee can become a reliable resource in Margamukti Village. 

As a first step, this community service activity lays the foundation for a sustainable program that, in the future, is expected to expand beyond outreach and include technical assistance, post-harvest training, and robust marketing strategies. Thus, Margamukti Village can serve as an example of a village that has successfully developed specialty coffee as a local economic driver while contributing to sustainable development.
