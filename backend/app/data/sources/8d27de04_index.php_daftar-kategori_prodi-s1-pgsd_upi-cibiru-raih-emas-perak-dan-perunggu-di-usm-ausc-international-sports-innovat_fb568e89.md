UPI Cibiru Wins Gold, Silver, and Bronze Medals at the USM–AUSC International Sports Innovation Challenge 2025: Evidence of Contribution to SDGs 3, 4, and 9

Pulau Pinang, January 2025 — The Indonesian University of Education (UPI) Cibiru Campus has once again achieved a brilliant achievement on the international stage. At the USM–AUSC International Sports Innovation Challenge 2025, held at the Setia Spice Arena, Pulau Pinang, Malaysia, UPI Cibiru innovators won gold, silver, and bronze medals in various categories, affirming the campus's position as a globally competitive center for innovation in sports, science, and technology.

This international competition, organized by Universiti Sains Malaysia (USM) in collaboration with the Asian University Sports Council (AUSC), brings together participants from various countries, including researchers, sports professionals, coaches, and students. With the theme "Empowering Innovation, Performance, and Health Through Sports Science, Engineering, and Technology," the competition provides a strategic platform for the development of visionary ideas that will impact the future of sports science and health technology.

UPI Cibiru on the International Stage

The competition showcased innovation in four key areas—sports science, engineering, healthcare, and digital education. Amidst the intense competition, UPI Cibiru's medal win is not only an award but also concrete evidence that this campus:

have mature research capacity,

giving birth to impactful innovations,

showcasing the best potential of Indonesian educators, researchers, and students.

UPI Cibiru's presence in this international event also expands global collaboration networks, opens up opportunities for joint research development, and strengthens academic diplomacy in the Asian region.

Contribution to SDGs

UPI Cibiru's achievements in this competition have strategic relevance to the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), especially:

1. SDGs 3 — Good Health and Well-being

Innovations in sports, health engineering, and human body technology contribute to improving people's health, fitness, and athletic performance.

2. SDGs 4 — Quality Education

The participation of educators and students in international events is evidence of the improvement in the quality of higher education, especially in applied research and innovative learning based on science and technology.

3. SDGs 9 — Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure

The innovative works presented at this competition encourage the birth of new scientific solutions that support the development of the sports industry, engineering technology, and strengthening the national innovation ecosystem.

Making a Trace of Success on the World Stage

This achievement confirms that UPI Cibiru excels not only in education but also as a significant force in innovation in sports, technology, and science at the international level. Winning medals in various categories marks UPI Cibiru's confident progress, expanding its global network, and achieving national glory.

With this achievement, UPI Cibiru has once again proven its capacity as a globally competitive innovation campus—bringing new ideas, concrete solutions, and inspiration for the development of education and sports in Indonesia and the world.
