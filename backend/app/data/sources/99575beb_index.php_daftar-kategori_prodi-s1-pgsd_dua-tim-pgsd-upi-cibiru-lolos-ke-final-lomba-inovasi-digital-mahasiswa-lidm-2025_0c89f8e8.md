Two UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education Teams Qualify for the 2025 Student Digital Innovation Competition (LIDM) Finals

Bandung, November 2025 — Students of the Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) Study Program at UPI Cibiru Campus have once again achieved a proud achievement. Two teams, Team Arsantara and Team Aradewa, have successfully qualified as finalists in the 2025 Student Digital Innovation Competition (LIDM) in the Digital Education Microteaching Division. Both teams will represent the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) in the final round, which will be held at PGRI Adi Buana University in Surabaya from December 1–4, 2025.

Those who qualified were the Arsantara Team, led by Siti Hawa (2206835) , with members Ayu Rahmawati (2207420), Kharisma Salsabila Sofa (2209612), and Nabilahthul Fuadah (2203828). Team leader Siti Hawa expressed her gratitude for this opportunity.

"We never expected to make it to the final stage. This process has taught us that collaboration and dedication are the keys to innovation. We're ready to give our best and further enhance UPI's reputation in the digital education innovation competition," he said.

Meanwhile, another team, the Aradewa team, is led by Riesma Aulia Salsabila (2204386) , with members Nurul Mutia Ramdhani (2203790), Rif'an Fazrin Zulfikar (2308845), and Sizka Amelia Febrianti (2303677). The Aradewa team leader, Riesma Aulia Salsabila, also expressed her enthusiasm ahead of the final round.

"Becoming a LIDM finalist is a valuable experience. We hope our innovation in digital microteaching can contribute to the development of adaptive and creative learning. This motivates us to continue innovating," he said.

 

Supervisor Support

Both teams were guided by Ranu Sudarmansyah, M.Pd., who also expressed his appreciation for the achievements of the students under his guidance.

"I'm proud to see the students' dedication and creativity. They demonstrate that future teachers must be able to integrate technology and pedagogy innovatively. This success is the first step toward achieving success at the national level," said Ranu.

UPI's Steps in Encouraging Educational Innovation

The success of these two UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) teams demonstrates the study program's commitment to fostering a culture of innovation, particularly in the realm of digital microteaching, a crucial competency for 21st-century teachers. LIDM is a national competition aimed at encouraging students to create technology-based creative works in education.

This achievement is expected to inspire other students to continue developing their digital, pedagogical, and collaborative skills in facing today's educational challenges.
