The PGPAUD Study Program at UPI Cibiru Campus Holds a STEAM Practitioner Activity with PT Indonesia Intelectual Academy

Cibiru, December 10, 2024 – The Early Childhood Education Teacher Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) in Cibiru successfully held a practical activity themed Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics (STEAM). This event was held in collaboration with PT Indonesia Intelectual Academy, an industry partner that has long collaborated with the study program. The activity took place over two days, on December 10 and 11, 2024, with the active participation of lecturers and all students.

PT Indonesia Intelectual Academy, known as a provider of innovative solutions in technology-based education, brought in practitioners to provide insights into the application of the STEAM approach in early childhood education. In the presentation, the practitioners explained the importance of STEAM as a holistic learning approach that integrates various disciplines to develop 21st-century skills in children.

"STEAM is not just a teaching method, but also a way to foster creativity, critical thinking, and problem-solving skills in children from an early age," said a practitioner from PT Indonesia Intelectual Academy.

In addition to theory sessions, students are encouraged to participate in practical activities involving designing STEAM-based projects. Some engaging activities include easy-to-do science experiment simulations in early childhood education classrooms, the use of technology-based tools for learning mathematics, and creative arts explorations that combine elements of engineering and technology.

The Head of the Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at UPI Cibiru Campus expressed his appreciation for the implementation of this activity. "The collaboration with PT Indonesia Intelectual Academy provides a very beneficial experience for students. They not only learn theory but also gain hands-on experience relevant to the world of modern education," he said.

The students in attendance expressed their enthusiasm for the activity. One said, "This activity has opened up new horizons for us. I've learned how STEAM can be applied practically to make early childhood education more engaging and effective."

With the conclusion of this activity, the Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at UPI Cibiru Campus continues to demonstrate its commitment to providing quality and relevant learning through collaboration with industry partners. The STEAM approach is expected to become a crucial foundation in developing creative and innovative PAUD educators to face the challenges of education in the digital era.
