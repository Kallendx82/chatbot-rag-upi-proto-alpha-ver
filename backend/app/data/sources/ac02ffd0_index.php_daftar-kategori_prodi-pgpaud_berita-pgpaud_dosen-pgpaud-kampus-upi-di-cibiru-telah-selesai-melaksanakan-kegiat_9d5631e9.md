Lecturers at the Early Childhood Education Program (PGPAUD) at the UPI Campus in Cibiru have completed the Therapeutic Play Skills training program.

Cibiru, May 17, 2025 — In order to improve understanding and skills in supporting early childhood development, Mrs. Ririn Despriliani, S.Pd., M.Si., as a lecturer in the PGPAUD Study Program at UPI Cibiru Campus, has participated in the Therapeutic Play Skills Training which took place from September 26, 2024 to May 17, 2025. This training was designed to deepen the practice of play therapy which aims to help children express themselves, recognize and manage emotions, and develop comprehensive social and cognitive skills. During the training process, participants were involved in various therapeutic approaches that were fun and meaningful for children.

The various activities carried out in this training include:

Sand play, where children create imaginative worlds using sand trays and miniature toys;

Playing with masks, by making two types of masks as a representation of external expression and internal feelings;

Role playing, to explore emotions through portraying certain characters;

Therapeutic storytelling, through the reading of meaningful stories by the therapist;

Creative visualization, by inviting children to imagine in a guided manner;

Arts and crafts, such as drawing, sculpture, or collage as a medium for self-expression;

Playing music and dancing, to train emotional expression as well as motor and social skills.

This training is expected to enrich the insights of educators and therapists, especially in implementing the play therapy approach as an effective and enjoyable method to support children's emotional development.

With the active involvement of PGPAUD lecturers in this training, it is hoped that the implementation of play therapy practices in early childhood education environments will be more optimal and have a positive impact on children's holistic growth and development.
