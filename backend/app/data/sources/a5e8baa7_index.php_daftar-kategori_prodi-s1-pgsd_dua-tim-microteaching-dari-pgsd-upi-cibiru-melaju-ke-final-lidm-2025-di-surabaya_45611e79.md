Two Microteaching Teams from PGSD UPI Cibiru Advance to the Finals of LIDM 2025 in Surabaya, Realizing Sustainable Learning Innovation (SDGs 4 & 17)

Bandung, December 1, 2025 — The two best teams from the Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) Study Program at UPI Cibiru Campus officially participated in the final round of the 2025 Student Digital Innovation Competition (LIDM) in the Digital Education Microteaching Division. The national competition, held from December 1–4, 2025, took place at PGRI Adi Buana University in Surabaya and brought together students from various universities to compete in technology-based learning innovations.

The two teams representing UPI Cibiru are the Arsantara Team and the Aradewa Team, which previously successfully passed the national selection stage thanks to the creativity and innovation of digital microteaching they developed. The Arsantara Team is led by Siti Hawa (2206835) with members Ayu Rahmawati (2207420), Kharisma Salsabila Sofa (2209612), and Nabilahthul Fuadah (2203828). Meanwhile, the Aradewa team is led by Riesma Aulia Salsabila (2204386) and Nurul Mutia Ramdhani (2203790), Rif'an Fazrin Zulfikar (2308845), Sizka Amelia Febrianti (2303677) as members. Both teams are guided by Ranu Sudarmansyah, M.Pd., a lecturer in PGSD UPI Cibiru.

Impressions and Expectations of the Participants

Siti Hawa, the head of the Arsantara Team, expressed her pride and positive challenges in participating in the national level competition:

"LIDM has provided us with valuable experience in innovating and developing relevant digital learning. We hope this work will be beneficial and widely used in education."

Meanwhile, the head of the Aradewa Team, Riesma Aulia Salsabila, added:

"Making it to the finals is a huge accomplishment. We learned how to combine technology, pedagogy, and creativity in learning. This experience in Surabaya was truly eye-opening."

Supervising lecturer, Ranu Sudarmansyah, M.Pd., gave his appreciation and hopes:

"Both teams have demonstrated extraordinary dedication. LIDM is not just a competition, but a platform to prepare prospective teachers who are innovative and adaptable to digital transformation. I hope this experience strengthens their competencies as future educators."
