PGPAUD Cibiru Campus and LPD DSTI Hold Coding and Artificial Intelligence Training for PAUD Teachers in Bandung City

Cibiru, September 28, 2025 — Lecturers in the Early Childhood Education Teacher Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at the Indonesian University of Education, Cibiru Campus, in collaboration with LPD DSTI (Digital Science and Technology Innovation), held a Coding and Artificial Intelligence (KA/AI) training for PAUD teachers in Bandung City. This activity is an effort to strengthen the competency of PAUD educators so they can face the rapidly evolving demands of 21st-century learning.

The training, attended by early childhood education (PAUD) teachers from various institutions in Bandung, focused on introducing basic coding concepts, utilizing AI for early learning, and integrating simple technologies into classroom play-learning activities. Lecturers from PGPAUD UPI Cibiru, along with a team from LPD DSTI, provided guidance, from theory to hands-on practice, enabling participants to understand and apply technology effectively.

The training was interactive, with teachers learning to create simple game-based coding activities, develop digital learning media, and understand how technology can be used to enhance cognitive development in early childhood. Participants welcomed the training, which provided new insights relevant to today's educational transformation.

This collaboration between PGPAUD UPI Cibiru Campus and LPD DSTI is an important step in promoting digital literacy in the PAUD ecosystem, while also emphasizing the role of higher education in providing a direct impact on improving the quality of education in the community.

This activity supports SDG 4: Quality Education, specifically in the areas of improving teacher competency and providing inclusive, relevant, and future-oriented education. By equipping early childhood education (PAUD) teachers with digital skills and the use of AI, this training contributes to quality learning that is responsive to technological developments. Furthermore, this activity also strengthens SDG target 4.7, namely education that promotes 21st-century skills, innovation, and technological literacy for all educators and students.
