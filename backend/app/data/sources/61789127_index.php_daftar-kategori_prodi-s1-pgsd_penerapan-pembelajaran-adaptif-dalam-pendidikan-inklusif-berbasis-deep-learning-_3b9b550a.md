Implementation of Adaptive Learning in Inclusive Education Based on Deep Learning at Labschool Kindergarten UPI Cibiru

The Community Service Program (PkM) based on scientific expertise with the title "Implementation of Adaptive Learning in Inclusive Education Based on the Deep Learning Approach at UPI Cibiru Labschool Kindergarten" was held on Monday, August 25, 2025. This activity is part of the competitive grant for lecturers at the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) in 2025 with the proposing chairperson Triana Lestari, S.Psi., M.Pd. , lecturer in the Elementary School Teacher Education Study Program, UPI Cibiru Campus, in collaboration with the UPI Campus Laboratory Kindergarten in Cibiru.

In her presentation, Triana explained the results of research on adaptive teaching syntax and its relevance to deep learning . According to her, deep learning can be measured through behavioral indicators derived from the concept of children's executive functions. This function plays a role in directing adaptive and goal-directed behavior, while also helping children delay automatic responses in problem-solving—a concrete manifestation of deep learning .

This adaptive learning syntax was introduced as the primary framework to support inclusive education, particularly in improving teacher competency through structured training. During the activity, teachers and helpers were trained to recognize good practices of in-depth learning through the adapteach syntax, which has proven effective based on the results of lesson studies with student interns. The measurement results showed that in addition to a significant increase in student learning ownership, teachers' self-efficacy from pre-training to post-training conditions experienced a significant increase, indicating that teacher training has a strong impact on improving their self-efficacy.

The program doesn't stop at the training stage. The implementation of adaptive learning syntax will continue in the classroom for the next semester. Triana emphasized the importance of reflection and discussion on implementation results as a form of ongoing collaboration between lecturers and school partners.

Dewi Sri Sugiharti, S.Pd., Principal of Labschool Kindergarten UPI Cibiru, stated that the teachers and principal have enthusiastically welcomed the program. "We hope that the collaborative implementation of this program will run smoothly and produce optimal impacts for teacher competency development and children's learning," she said.
