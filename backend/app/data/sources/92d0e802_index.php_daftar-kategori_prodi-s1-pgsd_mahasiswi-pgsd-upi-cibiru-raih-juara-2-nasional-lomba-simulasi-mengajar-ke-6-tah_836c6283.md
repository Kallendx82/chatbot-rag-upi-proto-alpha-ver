UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education Student Wins Second Place in the 6th National Teaching Simulation Competition in 2025: Realizing Quality Education SDGs

Bandung, 2025 — Students of the Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) Study Program at the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) Cibiru Campus have once again achieved a proud achievement. Ervina Puji Nurfajri, a PGSD student, won second place at the national level in the 6th Teaching Simulation Competition in 2025 in the Classroom Teacher category, outperforming hundreds of participants from various universities across Indonesia.

The competition attracted 279 participants from 28 universities, making it one of the largest and most competitive pedagogical competitions nationally. Ervina's achievement strongly indicates that UPI Cibiru's Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) students possess the pedagogical competence, learning creativity, and professional acumen to compete in the modern world of education.

Participant Statement

In a short interview, Ervina Puji Nurfajri expressed her gratitude and motivation after successfully becoming the second best finalist.

"I am very grateful to have won second place in such a major event. This competition has been a valuable experience in honing my creative and professional teaching skills. I hope this achievement will motivate others to maintain their confidence and develop their potential as future teachers," said Ervina.

Supervisor's Statement

Ervina's achievements cannot be separated from the guidance of Dr. Dede Trie Kurniawan, S.Si., M.Pd., a lecturer at PGSD UPI Cibiru and also the competition supervisor.

"Ervina has demonstrated dedication and strong character as a prospective professional teacher. This success proves that PGSD students have the capacity to compete at the national level. We will continue to support students in developing their pedagogical skills, creativity, and learning innovation," said Dr. Dede.
