PGPAUD UPI Cibiru Campus Participates in the Sakuranesia Society Seminar: Reflections and Modernization of AI- and STEAM-Based Education

Cibiru, May 20, 2025 - The Early Childhood Education Teacher Education Study Program (PGPAUD) of the UPI Campus in Cibiru participated in the Sakuranesia Society Seminar: Reflection and Modernization of AI and STEAM-Based Education, which was held on May 20, 2025. This seminar is part of the Road to The 6th International Conference on Primary and Early Childhood Education (ICPECE) 2025 series, which carries the main theme of technology-based educational innovation, global collaboration, and preservation of human values.

This activity presents international speakers and industry practitioners who are influential in educational transformation, including:

Sakura Ijuin (Founder of Sakuranesia), who emphasizes the urgency of preserving local culture as a foundation in developing meaningful and character-based educational innovations.

Prof. Koji Kurusu, MD, Ph.D. (Founder & CEO of Azalee Group), who explained the potential of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in improving the quality of education and healthcare services, as well as the importance of cross-sector collaboration to create a strong innovation ecosystem.

Tofik, ST (Co-Founder of Sakuranesia), who shared the practice of developing a sustainable innovation ecosystem based on technology and community collaboration.

By participating in this seminar, PGPAUD UPI Cibiru affirmed its commitment to strengthening the capacity of students and faculty in the era of AI- and STEAM-based learning. The speakers' presentations broadened participants' understanding of the importance of integrating technology with cultural preservation, while also emphasizing that educational innovation must remain aligned with humanitarian values.

This activity also directly contributes to the achievement of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), particularly SDG 4: Quality Education, through efforts to improve the quality of learning that adapts to technological developments and global needs. This seminar reinforces UPI Cibiru's commitment to providing inclusive, competitive, and relevant education to meet the demands of the digital era. Furthermore, the spirit of international collaboration within this activity supports the achievement of transformative learning and sustainability in education.

In keeping with the momentum of National Awakening Day, this seminar serves as a reminder that awakening in the modern era is not only about the spirit of unity, but also the courage to innovate. Through international collaboration, digital literacy, and the use of technology for educational advancement, UPI Cibiru is committed to continuously moving forward towards a smarter, more humane, and more sustainable future.
