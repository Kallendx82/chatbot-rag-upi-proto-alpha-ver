UPI Cibiru Campus Elementary School Teacher Education Lecturers Participate in Online ToT Training for Prospective Teachers in the PIP Training Program

Bandung – Two lecturers from the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) Cibiru Campus, Dr. Agil Nanggala, M.Pd. and Ranu Sudarmansyah, M.Pd., were officially assigned to participate in the Training of Trainers (ToT) for Prospective Teachers of the Pancasila Ideology Development Training (PIP) Primary Qualification Class of 2024. This activity took place online via Zoom Meeting and the Learning Management System (LMS) on September 23–26, 2025.

The training organized by the Pancasila Ideology Development Agency (BPIP) Deputy for Education and Training is a follow-up to BPIP Regulation Number 2 of 2024 concerning Pancasila Ideology Development Education and Training, as well as the Decree of the Head of BPIP Number 60 of 2024 concerning Technical Instructions for PIP Training Teacher Certification.

The ToT participants, including lecturers from UPI's Cibiru Campus, have previously passed the certification stage and are now entering the final stage, intensive training as prospective teachers. Over four days, they will be provided with basic and general material, as well as Pancasila enrichment to support their professionalism in teaching at the PIP training.

In his statement, Dr. Agil Nanggala, M.Pd., stated that this activity provided valuable experience and strengthened his commitment as an educator. "Through this ToT, I am increasingly convinced that the role of lecturers is not only to transfer knowledge but also to instill national values. This is an important opportunity to expand our contribution to fostering the Pancasila ideology," he said.

Meanwhile, Ranu Sudarmansyah, M.Pd., emphasized the importance of this training's relevance to elementary education. "Pancasila values ​​must be instilled from an early age through learning. With the knowledge gained from this ToT, we can more optimally integrate Pancasila ideology into educational practices in elementary schools," he said.

In his letter of assignment, the Director of the UPI Cibiru Campus, Prof. Dr. H. Karim Suryadi, M.Sc., expressed his hope that the lecturers would participate diligently and report their findings upon completion of the training. "The involvement of UPI Cibiru lecturers in this ToT is an important part of strengthening the role of academics in fostering Pancasila ideology across various educational sectors," he said.

With this participation, UPI Cibiru Campus is actively committed to supporting the national program for fostering the Pancasila ideology, while also expanding the contribution of lecturers in strengthening the nation's character through formal and non-formal education.
