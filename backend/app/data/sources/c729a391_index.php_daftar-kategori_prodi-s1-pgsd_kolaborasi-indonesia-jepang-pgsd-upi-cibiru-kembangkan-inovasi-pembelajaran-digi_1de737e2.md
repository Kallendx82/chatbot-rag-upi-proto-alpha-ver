Indonesia–Japan Collaboration: UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education Program Develops Digital Learning Innovations with Hiroshima University

CIBIRU, June 8, 2026 – The Elementary School Teacher Education Study Program (PGSD) of the Indonesian University of Education, Cibiru Campus, together with SD Labschool UPI Cibiru, held an international collaborative activity entitled “Empowering Future Generations” which featured a team from Hiroshima University, Japan, namely  Assoc. Professor Takamichi Asakura, Ph.D. and Dr. Taniguchi . This activity is part of an effort to strengthen international cooperation in the field of elementary education while expanding the learning experience of all students, teachers, and students.

Through a collaboration between UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) lecturers, Labschool elementary school teachers, and a team from Hiroshima University , this activity provided an interactive space that brought together various educational perspectives from Indonesia and Japan. Participants engaged in discussions, observations, and learning practices that conveyed the importance of global collaboration in preparing future generations.

One of the main agenda items was the implementation and introduction of Suralanet , a digital learning platform used as part of ongoing educational research. During the event, Hiroshima University faculty, along with UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) lecturers, conducted observations and exchanged ideas on the use of digital technology to support a more interactive, inclusive learning process focused on the needs of 21st-century learners.

This activity not only served as a platform for exchanging knowledge among teachers but also provided valuable experience for students. With great enthusiasm, the students participated in various activities designed to foster communication skills, collaboration, creativity, and adaptability in a multicultural environment. Direct interaction with Japanese academics and participants provided a unique learning experience and broadened their horizons regarding the global world of education.

For the UPI Cibiru Elementary Teacher Education Department (PGSD), this activity demonstrates the program's commitment to strengthening the internationalization of education through academic collaboration, research, and the development of learning innovations. The presence of partners from Hiroshima University opens up opportunities for expanding collaboration in teacher education, joint research, academic exchanges, and the development of learning models relevant to today's educational challenges.

Through the spirit of “Empowering Future Generations” , PGSD UPI Cibiru and Hiroshima University demonstrate that cross-country collaboration can be an effective means of creating educational innovation while building a young generation that has global insight, collaborative skills, and readiness to face future challenges.

The event concluded with a joint reflection and documentation session, marking the strengthening academic relationship between the Elementary School Teacher Education Department (PGSD) of UPI Cibiru, Elementary School Labschool of UPI Cibiru, and Hiroshima University, Japan. It is hoped that this collaboration will continue and contribute positively to the development of quality basic education at the national and international levels.
