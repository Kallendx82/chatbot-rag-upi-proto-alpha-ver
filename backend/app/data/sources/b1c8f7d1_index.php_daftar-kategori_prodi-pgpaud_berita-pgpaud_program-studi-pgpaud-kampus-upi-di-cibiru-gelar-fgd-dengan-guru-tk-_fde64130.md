The PGPAUD Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru Holds a Focus Group Discussion (FGD) with Kindergarten Teachers and Industry Partners to Formulate a Field-Based Curriculum

Cibiru, November 30, 2024 – The Early Childhood Education Teacher Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at the Indonesian University of Education (UPI) Campus in Cibiru successfully held a Focus Group Discussion (FGD) with kindergarten teachers and industrial company partners on November 29-30, 2024. This activity aims to formulate a curriculum that is relevant and responsive to the needs of the early childhood education (AUD) world and industry demands.

The FGD involved several early childhood education (PAUD) teachers who have partnered with the PGPAUD Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru, as well as representatives from partner companies operating in the early childhood sector. The discussion focused on identifying the competencies graduates must possess to face challenges in the workplace and society.

The Head of the Early Childhood Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru stated that this activity is part of an effort to implement the "link and match" principle in the higher education curriculum. "We want to ensure that the PGPAUD curriculum not only meets academic standards but is also relevant to practical needs in the field, both in education and industry," he said.

During the discussion, kindergarten teachers expressed the need for graduates with skills in digital-based classroom management, the use of interactive learning media, and strong communication skills with parents. Meanwhile, industry partners highlighted the importance of technology mastery and innovation in developing learning programs.

This FGD produced several strategic recommendations, including:

Strengthening material related to digital literacy and technology in the curriculum.
Addition of student collaboration programs with industry to improve understanding of field practices.
Increased focus on soft skills such as creativity, leadership, and collaboration.

With the results of this FGD, the PGPAUD Study Program at the UPI Campus in Cibiru hopes to present a curriculum that is not only relevant but also adaptive to current developments. "We are committed to continuing to collaborate with teachers and industry partners to produce superior graduates who are ready to compete globally," concluded the Head of the Study Program.
