UPI Computer Engineering Students at the Cibiru Campus Achieve Finalist Status in the 2025 LIDM Competition

The extended family of the UPI Computer Engineering Study Program, Cibiru Campus, proudly congratulates:

* Muhammad Taufik Dwi Putra, S.Tr.Kom., MTI (Assistant Lecturer)
* Nur Adi Supriyantomo
* Muhamad Daffa Adika Utama
* Muhammad Zaki Fadilah

for his achievement in becoming a Finalist in the 2025 Student Digital Innovation Competition (LIDM) through the SPORA Team in the Educational Digital Video Division (VDP) .

This success demonstrates the team's creativity, commitment, and dedication in delivering innovative work in the digital field. This achievement also demonstrates that UPI Cibiru Computer Engineering students are capable of competing and achieving at the national level.

Hopefully, this achievement will motivate us to continue working, innovating, and bringing the good name of the Indonesian Education University to various prestigious events in the future.

The news has been linked to the SDGs:

DG 4 – Quality Education

Through the digital innovations they develop, the team presents solutions that can improve access to and the quality of learning. Digital media, educational applications, or interactive systems are designed to help students and the community acquire knowledge in a more inclusive manner.

SDG 9 – Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure

The UPI team's work demonstrates their ability to design modern, efficient, and adaptive technology to industrial developments. This aligns with efforts to build innovation-based digital infrastructure as a foundation for national industrial transformation.

SDG 10 – Reducing Inequality

By providing learning platforms or digital services that are accessible to a wide range of people, their innovations have the potential to bridge the digital divide between urban communities and more remote areas.

SDG 17 – Partnerships to Achieve the Goals

The success of reaching the final round of LIDM is inseparable from the collaboration between students, supervising lecturers, the university, and the digital community. This synergy reflects the importance of multi-stakeholder collaboration in advancing the sustainable development agenda.
