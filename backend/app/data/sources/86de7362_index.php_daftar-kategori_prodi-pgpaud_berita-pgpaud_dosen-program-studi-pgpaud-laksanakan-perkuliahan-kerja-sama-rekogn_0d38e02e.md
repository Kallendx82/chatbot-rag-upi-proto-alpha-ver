Lecturers of the Early Childhood Education Study Program Conduct Collaborative Lectures on Recognizing Prior Learning in East Kutai

The Early Childhood Education Teacher Education (PGPAUD) Study Program at UPI Cibiru Campus held a Collaborative Lecture on Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) in East Kutai as a way to strengthen regional partnership-based education services. The online event was attended by 41 students out of a total of 52 registered RPL participants.

The course, "Early Childhood Physical Motor Development," discusses the concepts, principles, and strategies for stimulating gross and fine motor skills in children. Students gain a deep understanding of developing developmentally appropriate physical activities and applying effective learning in early childhood education settings.

The implementation of this activity also represents a concrete contribution of PGPAUD to the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), particularly SDG 4: Quality Education. Through the RPL program, PGPAUD strives to expand access to education for communities in various regions, recognize previous learning experiences, and provide an inclusive, flexible, and quality-improving learning system. The online implementation also supports equal learning opportunities without geographical boundaries, so that students from East Kutai can continue to optimally participate in the educational process.

The PGPAUD Study Program of UPI Cibiru Campus hopes that this collaboration will continue to strengthen the quality of learning, improve student competencies, and support the equal distribution of quality education for various levels of society in Indonesia.
