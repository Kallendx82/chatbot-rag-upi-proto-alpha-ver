UPI Cibiru Campus Clean Friday: Cleaning Staff Work Together and Care About Maintaining the Campus Environment

UPI Cibiru Campus reaffirmed its commitment to maintaining a clean and beautiful environment through a Clean Friday activity held on roadsides and throughout the campus. Five campus janitors participated in the activity, demonstrating a strong sense of community and environmental stewardship. All activities were led by Dede Irawan, the field coordinator, as part of the implementation of the green campus culture program.

Cleaning roadsides, parks, and open spaces on campus has a direct impact on improving the comfort of the academic community, enhancing the aesthetics of the environment, and creating a more beautiful campus atmosphere. A well-maintained environment is also a crucial factor in supporting learning productivity and institutional activities.

Furthermore, this activity also strengthened a culture of mutual cooperation and shared concern for campus facilities. The close-knit teamwork of the cleaning staff exemplifies a collaborative work culture that positively impacts the quality of cleaning services and the image of a greener, more humane campus.

This activity also supports SDG 3 (Healthy and Well-Being) by creating a healthy environment, and SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities) by managing clean and environmentally friendly campus spaces. Thus, Clean Friday is a concrete contribution from UPI Cibiru Campus in supporting the development of a sustainable green campus.
