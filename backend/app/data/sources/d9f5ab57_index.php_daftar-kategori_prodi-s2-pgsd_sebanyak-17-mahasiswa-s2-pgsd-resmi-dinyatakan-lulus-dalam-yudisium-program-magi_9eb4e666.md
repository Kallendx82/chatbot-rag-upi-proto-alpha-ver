A total of 17 Master of Elementary School Teacher Education (PGSD) students were officially declared to have graduated from the Master's Program Graduation Ceremony.

The Master of Elementary School Teacher Education (S2 PGSD) Program at the Indonesian University of Education (UIPI) Cibiru Campus held a graduation ceremony to mark the end of the academic process for students. Seventeen students were declared graduates and officially received their master's degrees after completing all courses, research, and thesis writing in accordance with applicable academic regulations.

This graduation ceremony marks a crucial moment in the students' academic journey and reflects the success of the educational process within the Elementary School Teacher Education Program (PGSD). Graduates are expected to be able to apply their scientific competencies, leadership skills, and professional skills to the development of basic education across various contexts. This aligns with the study program's commitment to producing educators and education practitioners who are adaptable to the dynamics of the times and the needs of society.

This graduation ceremony also aligns with Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) point 4, namely Quality Education, which emphasizes the importance of improving the quality of educators as a primary foundation for human development. The presence of the Master of Elementary School Teacher Education graduates is expected to contribute to strengthening the quality of learning, developing educational innovations, and continuously improving the capacity of elementary school teachers.

Through this graduation ceremony, the UPI Cibiru Elementary School Teacher Education Masters Study Program affirms its commitment to producing graduates who are not only academically superior, but also have a concern for sustainable development, character, and are able to become agents of change in the world of elementary education in Indonesia.
